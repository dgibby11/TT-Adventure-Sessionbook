Overview

The Rangers Department maintains a mews of trained falcons used for field communication on extended operations. Fourteen birds are on the current register. Each is trained for specific routes and specific handlers — a falcon trained to Handler A will not reliably return to Handler B, and the training does not transfer. The system functions well when the handler roster is stable.

The Missing Birds

Three falcons are currently unaccounted for. Two have been missing long enough — six weeks and three months respectively — that they should have returned on their own even without a handler's recall. Trained birds return. These two have not. The third went missing eight days ago; this one may simply be delayed. The department has been tracking the gaps and has not yet filed an external report. The Rangers' threshold for filing is specific and has not been met. It is going to be met.

[[maren_duskhollow|Maren Duskhollow]] is associated with the Rangers by historical connection. The department is aware of this and has checked their records to see if the missing birds were on routes associated with her last known work. The check was inconclusive.

DM Only

The first two birds were intercepted. Not lost — intercepted by someone who knew the routes and the handler schedule well enough to redirect the birds during transit. The messages they were carrying were routine department correspondence, but one of them contained a route update that, combined with other recent department records, would give the recipient a comprehensive picture of the Rangers' current field operation areas. Someone wanted that information. The interception method is sophisticated and implies either a former department member or someone with access to department records.

The third bird may or may not be related. It was on a different route, assigned to a handler who is new this year. If it comes back, what it's carrying will clarify whether the incidents are connected.
