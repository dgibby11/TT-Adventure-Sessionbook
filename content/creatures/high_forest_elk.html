Overview

A herd of elk that has been crossing the campus grounds every autumn for longer than any living faculty member can remember. The crossing runs northeast to southwest and takes approximately two hours from the moment the first animals reach the perimeter wall to the last crossing into the western woodland. The route has not changed in any year the Academy has records for. The Rangers Department tracks arrival timing and posts notice. Traffic on the main crossing path is redirected during the crossing; this is standard procedure and students accept it without comment.

The Route

The route takes the herd directly through the [[druids_grove|Druids courtyard]], which requires the animals to move through an enclosed building space. The courtyard doors on the relevant axis are opened for them. The plants in the grove orient themselves before the herd arrives — the department has logged this consistently and does not have an explanation for how the plants know before the perimeter sightings. The elk move through the grove as if it is a familiar place, which it has been, for this herd, for every autumn of their lives.

DM Only

The route is old. It predates the Academy, predates the buildings, predates the planting of the grove. The [[grove_spirit|grove spirit]] is aware of the herd and expects them. The grove's anticipatory behavior before the crossing is the spirit orienting its space for their arrival, which it has done every year for longer than the Academy has existed. The herd's willingness to pass through the enclosed courtyard without distress is partly the spirit making the space feel correct to them.

One animal in the current herd — a large cow elk with an irregular gait, identifiable by a pale stripe on her left shoulder — has been crossing for longer than the typical lifespan of an elk. The Rangers department has noted her presence in records going back eighteen years. [[archdruid_sevra|Sevra]] has noted her in records going back thirty-one. Neither has flagged this discrepancy to the other. The animal is, as far as anyone has been able to determine, an ordinary elk.
