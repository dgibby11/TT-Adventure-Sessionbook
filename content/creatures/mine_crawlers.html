Overview

Something lives in the deeper shafts of the Rogues mine. It is not a student. It has not been identified from the sounds it makes, which are irregular — scraping at variable intervals, something that might be breathing at a depth that makes localization difficult, occasionally something that students have described as "reorganizing," though they have not described what is being reorganized.

It has not been aggressive. [[tavin_crestfall|Crestfall's]] journal refers to it as "the permanent tenant" and notes its presence at three specific junctions in the deep shafts without describing it. The journal was the most detailed documentation of the phenomenon that existed. The journal is currently inaccessible.

Encounter Record

No confirmed visual contact. Students who have reached the relevant sections of the deep shafts have reported the sounds, noted the movement in peripheral light, and — in four documented student accounts from different years — described independently arriving at the same decision, which was to proceed differently than they had planned. No student who has been in the deep shafts reports having been harmed. The number of students who returned to the deep shafts after their first visit is noticeably lower than the number who went the first time.

DM Only

The mine crawlers are offspring of the [[rogues_deep_shafts|permanent tenant]] — a creature that has been in the mine since before the Academy and before the miners. They are the permanent tenant's extended presence in the upper sections of the deep shafts. They have not been aggressive because the permanent tenant has not decided that anything in the shafts is worth aggression. This may change. The increased activity on the [[ritual_floor|ritual floor]] is registering to the permanent tenant as a change in the local conditions that it has been monitoring for a long time. The mine crawlers have been more active in the last two years than in the decade before. Crestfall noticed this. The journal contains this observation. It is the most urgent piece of information in the journal.
