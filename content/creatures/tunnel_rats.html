Overview

Large rats, unusually organized. Not aggressive to people who leave them alone. Students in the Rogues mine have been observing them for years and the consistent report is that the rats are sorting things — moving small objects from one location to another on a schedule that is not random. The schedule appears to be tied to time of day: certain movements happen between specific hours, other movements at other hours. No student has been able to determine what the sorting achieves.

The Sorting

The objects being moved are small and varied — pieces of stone, mineral fragments, occasional small metal items, organic material from the deeper sections of the mine. The destination sites appear to be specific locations in both the mine shafts and the underground storage corridors. Students who have followed the rats to their destination sites have found organized accumulations of material that do not look like nesting behavior. The accumulations are in spots that, on a map, form a pattern around the [[underground_storage|underground storage]] central chamber.

DM Only

The rats are responding to residual enchantment from the [[ritual_floor|ritual floor]]. The enchantment specifies, in its foundational logic, that the sites designated as anchor points should have specific material compositions maintained. The rats are not knowingly serving this function — they are responding to a low-level compulsion that the enchantment produces as a maintenance behavior, embedded in the magical field that permeates the underground levels. They have been doing this for decades. The accumulations they are building are slowly recreating the material components at the anchor points that were consumed or disturbed over time. At current progress, they would complete the restoration in approximately forty years. This will not be relevant on that timeline, but it demonstrates that the enchantment's maintenance protocols are still partially functional.
