Old tower on the north wall and the [[old_library|Old Library]] eaves

Overview

The campus ravens have been at FAIL Academy longer than anyone can reliably document. The first groundskeeping records mention them in the second decade of the institution's operation. Multiple attempts to relocate or discourage them have been unsuccessful — not aggressively, but persistently, in the manner of something that has decided this is where it lives and finds that position more durable than human inconvenience.

They are large, healthy birds. Their plumage is unusually consistent — very black, with a blue-green iridescence in direct sunlight. Several of the oldest individuals have noticeably pale patches around the eye and beak, which ravens do not normally develop. No one has definitively explained this.

Behavior

Ravens are intelligent birds. These ravens are intelligent in a way that students frequently notice and then try to find a mundane explanation for. They respond to their names — not names assigned to them by current students, but names they appear to have had before any current student arrived. The Rangers Department maintains an informal register of which names they respond to. The register currently has seven entries.

They are used informally as message carriers between departments, a practice that is not officially sanctioned and works anyway. Notes tied to a specific feather configuration reliably reach their destination. No one has established a mechanism for this. The Bards Department considers it a campus institution. The Wizards Department has twice attempted formal study. Both attempts produced inconclusive results and some very offended birds.

Notable Behaviors

  • They congregate at the [[memorial_garden|Founder's Memorial Garden]] on the Wednesday evenings that coincide with the new moon. Not always. Often enough to be noted.
  • [[torvald_thatch|Torvald Thatch]] has named seven individuals and speaks to them during his grounds rounds. They appear to listen.
  • Students who are observed doing something they shouldn't be — primarily in areas of campus they aren't supposed to be in — have reported a raven watching from a nearby perch. This may be coincidence.
  • They do not enter the [[underground_storage|basement access areas]]. Not reluctance — active avoidance. They land anywhere else on campus. They do not land near the food court service hatch.

DM Only

The ravens' unusual characteristics are real and not explained by mundane biology. Whatever they are — a bloodline effect, a druidic imprint from the founding era, something [[maren_duskhollow|Maren Duskhollow]] left behind — has not been established in this campaign.

Their avoidance of the basement access areas is an information flag. If PCs notice and ask Torvald about it, he will tell them it has been consistent for as long as he can remember, and that in his experience, animals that avoid a specific location are correct more often than the people who installed whatever is in it.

Mechanical note: A Druid or Ranger with appropriate abilities can communicate with them. They don't narrate events in human terms — they convey impressions, presences, and patterns. They have been on this campus for a very long time and they have noticed things.
