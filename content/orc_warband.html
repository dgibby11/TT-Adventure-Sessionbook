Overview

Orc warbands from the [[nether_mountains|Nether Mountains]] are a persistent threat to the Silver Marches trade routes and an occasional hazard for Academy field exercises in the mountain region. They are not a common encounter at the campus itself — the campus's reputation (and visible population of armed students) is a deterrent — but on the trade roads to [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]] and [[everlund|Everlund]], or anywhere north toward the mountains, contact is possible.

Recent reports from Everlund traders suggest warband activity in the Nether Mountain passes has increased significantly this year. The cause is not confirmed. Theories range from tribal displacement to organised raiding under new leadership to something more worrying that no one has named officially.

Standard Warband Composition

Scout party
3–5 orcs, possibly with a war dog; CR 1–2 total; hit fast, retreat if matched
Raiding party
8–12 orcs plus an Eye of Gruumsh (leader); CR 5–7 total; objective-focused
War party
15–20 orcs, Eye of Gruumsh, War Chief; CR 10+; doesn't raid — sieges

Tactics

  • Aggressive and direct — they do not typically use complex tactics unless a War Chief is present
  • Prioritise targets that are showing wounds (Aggressive trait: bonus action move toward a target)
  • Eyes of Gruumsh provide offensive buffs; killing the Eye first significantly reduces the warband's fighting effectiveness
  • Rarely pursue into terrain they don't know; retreat to higher ground if possible

DM Only

The increased warband activity in the [[nether_mountains|Nether Mountains]] is not purely organic. Someone has been arming and directing several formerly-independent warbands with a specific objective that has not yet become clear. [[prof_oswald_crumb|Prof. Crumb's]] research site in the mountains has been attacked twice in the past six months; he has not reported either incident to the Academy administration. He reported them to the [[thornwick_consortium|Thornwick Consortium]] instead.

Encounter scaling for the party (Level 5):

  • Scout party (4 orcs): Easy encounter — good for a surprise ambush on the road
  • Raiding party (10 orcs + Eye): Hard encounter — tactical challenge, especially if the Eye is allowed to act freely
  • War party: Deadly — use as a set piece or as something to escape from, not a standard fight
