Location
Far western campus, adjacent to the river
Focus
Primal rage, unarmored combat, endurance, instinct
Known for
Loudest department; highest disciplinary incident rate (eleven consecutive years)

Facilities

The main hall is low-ceilinged and wide, with exposed timber beams, hide tapestries along the walls, and open fire channels running down the center that are kept lit regardless of season or temperature. The outdoor sparring ring — packed earth, river stones at the perimeter, open sky — sees more traffic than the interior hall during waking hours. In winter, students train in it anyway. This is considered a feature, not a hardship.

The building's location against the river is deliberate. Running into the Rauvin and back is a standard warm-up exercise and an informal gauge of who is having a bad morning.

Culture

The Barbarians Department operates on a meritocracy that is more legible than most: you earn standing by doing impressive things in front of the people who matter, and the people who matter are whoever is watching. This produces a cohort with genuine camaraderie, spectacular informal competitions, and an institutional allergy to paperwork that drives the administrative wing to distraction.

Faculty here do not teach by lecturing. They demonstrate, they challenge, and occasionally they lose. A department head who can be beaten by a student is considered a success story, not an embarrassment — the student is expected to understand this means they are now the person setting the standard for the people below them.

Trophies

The hide tapestries lining the main hall are trophies — each one labeled with a name and a year going back to the Academy's early decades. The oldest one, near the back wall, has no label the current faculty can translate. The creature depicted is real, or was. Students who ask about it are told to get stronger first.
