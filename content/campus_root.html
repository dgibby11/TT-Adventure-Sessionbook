Overview

FAIL Academy occupies a dispersed campus on the northern fringe of Silverymoon — not a single compound but a collection of freestanding buildings spread across wooded grounds, connected by maintained paths. Its towers are visible from the River Rauvin. Each of the twelve departments occupies its own building, reached on foot from the central facilities. No department shares a wall with any other, and none is accessible from the Main Complex without crossing the open grounds.

The Academy's mandate, as stated in the founding documents, is to produce graduates who are formidable across the disciplines of arms, inquiry, and lore. The emphasis changes per headmistress. The current emphasis, under Dowe, is on graduates who are formidable and also know exactly what they're doing with that formidability. This is a more complex objective than it sounds.

Daily Life

Students live and train on campus for the duration of their enrollment. Departments operate on different schedules — the Fighters and Barbarians Departments begin before dawn; the Druids Department has a genuine midnight curriculum; the Rogues Department publishes no schedule at all. The Food Court operates on a fixed three-meal timeline that all twelve departments have theoretically agreed to work around and that approximately six of them actually do.

[[torvald_thatch|Torvald Thatch]] patrols the grounds before dawn and at dusk. After the Evening Bell, the campus belongs to whoever has a reason to be out in it. The institutional policy is that the lantern-posts along the main paths stay lit. The actual enforcement of what students do on those paths between dusk and curfew is more relaxed than the policy implies.

Dormitories

Most students are housed in the Main Complex dormitory wings rather than at their department buildings. Dormitory space at the department locations exists but is reserved primarily for upperclassmen — students who have demonstrated the level of commitment, or occasionally the logistical necessity, that justifies living on-site. Exceptions are made, usually by direct arrangement with a department head, and usually for reasons the administration does not advertise.

The practical effect is that first and second-year students cross the grounds between the central complex and their departments daily. This gives the main paths their constant foot traffic and is the primary reason most students have a working knowledge of which parts of the campus are and aren't monitored after dark.

The Departments

Twelve departments, each with its own building, culture, and relationship to the main academic administration. Students are assigned to departments at enrollment based on a combination of self-selection and an assessment process that the administration describes as "holistic evaluation" and that students describe as "somewhat opaque." Cross-department instruction is available but not structured. Students who train outside their department do so through direct arrangement with the relevant faculty, which is encouraged in principle and inconsistently supported in practice.

Security and Access

The central campus facilities have a perimeter wall, with monitored gates and one sealed entrance in the north face that appears on no current map. [[torvald_thatch|Torvald]] has the key to that gate. He has not been asked why by anyone in the last two decades. The department buildings on the surrounding grounds have no shared wall — they are freestanding, and access to the wider grounds is not formally controlled after the Evening Bell.

Campus security guards patrol the common grounds and central facilities. Their jurisdiction ends at department doors. Each department is responsible for its own access control, and the approaches they have taken vary considerably: locked mechanisms requiring specific skills, wards that respond to correct ritual action, a physical guard conducting a choreographed duel at the entrance. Students not enrolled in a department generally cannot enter without invitation or demonstration that they belong. The administration is aware of all of these systems and has never formally reviewed any of them.

The paths between the central complex and the department buildings are not lit after dark. Lantern-posts cover the central facilities and the main courtyard, but the walking routes out to the departments rely on ambient light and the knowledge students develop from making the trip repeatedly. This is widely considered a deliberate decision by someone, though no policy document says so.

The Missing Department

The Academy operates twelve departments. There is no Warlocks Department, and there has not been one since Year 23. The official record describes the closure as an "administrative restructuring." The unofficial record — available to anyone who asks the right faculty member after sufficient trust has been established — is that three consecutive cohorts of Warlock students had their benefactor arrangements turn out to be with entities that were, to varying degrees, working against the Academy's interests. The third incident involved a devil. The administration decided the enrollment model was not workable.

Students with warlock abilities are enrolled in other departments, typically Sorcerers, and advised to be straightforward about their arrangement with whoever they're studying under. The current approach is described as "case-by-case management." This is generally understood to mean that it works until it doesn't.

DM Only

The campus should feel like a place where important things are happening at the same time as mundane things — students who are stressed about exams while sitting above an ancient ritual chamber; faculty arguing about curricula while withholding institutional secrets. The ordinary and the dangerous share the same corridors. That's the register to play in.

Torvald is the players' most reliable non-faction ally for campus geography and history. He won't give them answers; he'll confirm what they've already figured out, if they've figured it out. He is also the only person on campus who knows where the sealed north gate leads and hasn't forgotten that it exists.
