Opening Hook

[[ellery_voss|Dr. Voss]] calls the party in after class. Praises how they handled themselves during the jungle breach despite the triceratops. Encourages them to find [[oswald_crumb|Professor Crumb]], who is assembling a small team for a three-day expedition to the [[nether_mountains|Nether Mountains]]. Hands them a letter of introduction — it magically admits them to his tower. Describes him as "eccentric but brilliant." She does not know about the Consortium.

Meeting Crumb

Goblin, 3'6", ink-stained fingers, disheveled robes, wire-rimmed spectacles. Corrects grammar constantly — even mid-crisis. Insists on "Professor Crumb" or "Oswald"; any attempt at "Oz" or "Ozzy" earns a short lecture. Catchphrases: "Precisely!" and "Now, where was I?" On the expedition's purpose: "Component gathering, exploration, documentation. The usual." Watch the lockbox — he places it near his bedroll and never lets it leave his sight.

First Tell — Night Watch

Camp at the mountain base, late afternoon Day 1. Temperature drops sharply at night. If anyone takes watch: Crumb checks his lockbox multiple times, counting something softly under his breath. He doesn't know he's being observed. Say nothing — let the players connect the dots later.

The Thornwick Reveal

Day 2, component gathering. Crumb's open field notes are visible: "Client Request: 50 units crystallized guano" and "Consortium deadline: harvest by mid-month." If pressed, he deflects ("Just thorough documentation") then cracks: "Fine! Yes, I sell components to the Thornwick Arcane Consortium. It's a perfectly legitimate side business! The university pays quite handsomely, and frankly, an academic's salary is laughable compared to—" He stops. On the lockbox: "Payment records and samples. Proprietary business, you understand. Now, shall we continue?"

Elemental Combat

Rocky ridge above 8,000 ft. Subtle tremors — Perception DC 11. Crumb absorbed in a stone outcropping. Ground ruptures: Large Earth Elemental + 3 Dust Mephits. Falling rocks = difficult terrain, dust cuts visibility, some component jars shatter. Crumb: oblivious until nearly hit — "What? Oh, is that an elemental? How fascinating!" Must be physically grabbed or he'll keep sketching. Each of his turns: gathers samples from the chaos. Casts Shield only if directly struck. Commentary: "Note the density of that creature! Remarkable structural integrity!" Post-fight: fills jars with elemental granite. Offers 5gp each as "danger pay" — let the party clock the insult against the 300gp Consortium value he mentioned. Reveals the blue dragon only if the mountain shakes or the party pushes him to leave.

Hidden Discovery

Perception DC 16 — any point during the mountain portion. On a success, a character spots a shape wedged in a crack in the rock face: a body, partially obscured, clearly not recent. Investigation reveals a deceased adventurer, no identifying marks still legible. On the body: a gold pouch (460 gp) and a battered but functional Shield of Missile Attraction — magical in nature, recognizable with an Arcana check. No note, no journal, nothing to explain why they were here alone.

Day 3 — Return

[[ellery_voss|Dr. Voss]] is waiting on campus (Crumb may have sent word ahead). She notices the party's state and asks for a report. Crumb's version: "Completely routine expedition, some minor elemental activity, components successfully acquired." Party can correct the record — Voss will be displeased with Crumb if they do. Payment: 50gp total from Crumb (negotiate higher). Components the party gathered are theirs. Exit line: "You're all still breathing, aren't you? I'd say the expedition was a success."

DM Only — Full Planning Notes

Setup

Dr. Voss praises the party's handling of the jungle midterm disaster (the triceratops) and encourages them to join [[oswald_crumb|Professor Crumb]]'s three-day expedition to the [[nether_mountains|Nether Mountains]] for component gathering. She provides a letter of introduction that magically admits them to Crumb's tower. She describes him as "eccentric but brilliant" — she does not know about his Consortium arrangement.

Crumb — Character Notes

Diminutive goblin, 3'6", ink-stained fingers, perpetually disheveled robes, wire-rimmed spectacles. Pedantic precision; corrects grammar constantly, even mid-crisis. Obsessed with cataloging and documentation.

  • Insists on "Professor Crumb" or "Oswald." Students who try "Oz" or "Ozzy" get a short lecture on formality.
  • Catchphrases: "Precisely!" and "Now, where was I?"
  • Takes meticulous notes on everything, including during combat.
  • Scattered demeanor masks a shrewd businessman. Despite shabby appearance, extremely wealthy — gold in concealed pouches and a lockbox he never lets leave his sight.
  • Terrible liar. Will eventually admit everything if pressed.

True purpose: The expedition is a supply run for the [[thornwick_consortium|Thornwick Arcane Consortium]], three weeks' travel east. He's using the party as paid bodyguards without disclosing the actual stakes. The priority target is powdered elemental granite, found only above 8,000 feet.

Day 1 — Travel & Camp

Depart at dawn. 12-mile journey north: cross the [[river_rauvin|River Rauvin]] by ferry or ford, then ascending rough terrain to the mountain foothills.

Travel complications (choose 1–2):

  • Merchant caravan crossing the Rauvin causes delays — Crumb complains about their "abysmal organizational system."
  • Foul weather (rain, wind) — Crumb obsesses over protecting his equipment, ignores the party getting soaked.
  • Campus Ranger patrol mocks the expedition as "a daytime field trip." Crumb delivers a cutting grammar correction in response.

Camp setup: Reach the mountain base by late afternoon. Safe enough. Temperature drops sharply at night.

Evening RP hooks: Crumb unpacks surveying tools, specimen jars, journals, and the lockbox (placed near his bedroll). He lectures on mountain geology while setting up. Critiques party tent stakes ("45 degrees, not 40 — the difference is critical"). Produces a surprisingly good dinner. If asked about the expedition's purpose: "Component gathering, exploration, documentation. The usual."

Night watch: Crumb checks his lockbox multiple times during the night, counting something softly.

Day 2 — The Expedition & Elemental Encounter

Crumb hands out collection satchels and specimen jars. His map of "promising sites" is suspiciously organized — quantities, grades, and packaging standards noted.

Components gathered (morning/early afternoon):

  • Crystallized bat guano — shallow caves and overhangs. Crumb examines each with a jeweler's loupe.
  • Petrified fungal spores — chipped from sedimentary rock layers with unnecessary geological commentary.
  • Lesser components — charcoal, phosphorus deposits, small diamonds in quartz, bird feathers, shamrock leaves, lodestones, thorny plant stems.

The discovery: Crumb's notes are visible — "Client Request: 50 units crystallized guano" and "Consortium deadline: harvest by mid-month." If confronted, he deflects ("Just thorough documentation") then cracks: "Fine! Yes, I sell components to the Thornwick Arcane Consortium. It's a perfectly legitimate side business! The university pays quite handsomely, and frankly, an academic's salary is laughable compared to—" He stops. If pressed on the lockbox: "Payment records and samples. Proprietary business. Now, shall we continue?"

Mid-afternoon push: Crumb insists they ascend further — the elemental granite only forms above 8,000 feet. Party objects (late, treacherous terrain); Crumb goes anyway: "I hired you for protection, not permission." They follow.

The Earth Elemental Encounter

Rocky ridge, exposed stone faces, loose scree. Subtle ground tremors (Perception DC 11 to notice). Crumb is immediately absorbed in an outcropping.

The ground rumbles — a fissure opens and a Large Earth Elemental emerges, triggered by excavation vibrations. Simultaneously, 3 Dust Mephits swirl up from the disturbed earth. Falling rocks cascade down the slope (difficult terrain, dust reduces visibility, some component jars shatter).

Crumb during combat: Doesn't notice until the first attack nearly hits him. "What? Oh, is that an elemental? How fascinating! I'm documenting the layer stratification here, which is absolutely—" Must be physically grabbed. Once combat starts, he uses his Action each turn to collect samples from the chaos. Casts Shield only if directly threatened. Running commentary: "Note the density of that creature! Remarkable structural integrity!"

Aftermath: Elemental remains yield pure powdered elemental granite. Crumb fills jars with reverent care while ignoring the party's exhaustion. If arguments start, the mountain shakes faintly — he admits the blue dragon that frequents nearby caves as a reason to leave immediately. "This haul alone will fetch 300 gold from the Consortium. Worth every moment of — well. You're all quite alive, which is fortunate." If pressed on recklessness: "I calculated the risk acceptable! Evidently, I was correct."

Second camp: Lower altitude. Crumb wraps jars obsessively. Offers 5 gold per party member as "danger pay" — they will notice the disparity with 300 gold mentioned.

Day 3 — Return

Slower return due to exhaustion. Cross the Rauvin again; arrive campus by late afternoon.

Dr. Voss is waiting (possibly alerted by Crumb sending a message ahead). She notices the party's state and demands a report. Crumb gives the sanitized version: "Completely routine expedition, some minor elemental activity, components successfully acquired." Party can correct the record.

Consequences & rewards:

  • Payment: 50 gold total from Crumb (party can negotiate higher).
  • Voss: Pleased they survived; concerned about Crumb's judgment if she learns the full picture.
  • Components: Party keeps what they gathered; can negotiate for the high-value specimens.
  • Reputation: Word spreads — capable enough to handle real danger. Some students approach with requests; others become wary.

Crumb's exit: Already arranging a caravan to transport the haul to the Consortium. Optional backhanded acknowledgment: "You handled yourselves adequately. The Consortium may have additional expeditions. I'll keep you in mind... if the pay negotiates favorably."

Key RP Beats

  • Grammar corrections at every opportunity ("It's 'I would have,' not 'I would of' — honestly, what are they teaching at this academy?")
  • The lockbox: he never lets it out of his sight; counting softly at night is the first tell
  • The realization scene: flustered defensiveness, talking too much, then stopping himself
  • Professional disconnect — he views the party as tools, isn't malicious, just genuinely oblivious
