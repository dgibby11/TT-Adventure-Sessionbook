Overview

Several wolf packs range the terrain around FAIL Academy, primarily along the [[river_rauvin|River Rauvin]] eastern bank and the open farmland northeast of campus. They are the most common wildlife encounter for students on field exercises and the most underestimated. A pack of six wolves attacking in coordination is a serious threat to a solo student; attacking in two flanking groups it can overwhelm an unprepared party.

Wolves near the Academy have had decades of exposure to armed students and are accordingly wary rather than bold. They do not attack groups of three or more unless the wolves are very hungry or the students are very unlucky. They absolutely attack individuals or pairs, especially after dark.

Pack Behaviour

  • Test prey by feinting and retreating; will not commit unless they have advantage of numbers
  • Pack Leader directs flanking — wolves rarely attack from only one side
  • Retreat if they take significant losses; not suicidal
  • Howl to communicate — distant howls during a night camp means a pack has located you and is considering options

Dire Wolves

Larger, more aggressive, and harder to discourage. One known dire wolf pack operates along the northern bank between mile markers 2 and 4. The [[expedition_club|Expedition Volunteers]] have documented their territory; the pack has been stable for three years. The Alpha is a scarred female with a missing left ear; she has encountered and withdrawn from student groups twice. She is considered territorial but rational.

DM Only — Tactical Notes

  • Wolf pack: 6 wolves, CR 1/4 each = CR 3 encounter for the party (easy-moderate at Level 5). Scale by adding dire wolves or a Pack Alpha (use Dire Wolf stats).
  • Standard wolf tactics: two wolves flank per target, one feints while the other bites. Prone targets are priority (they have advantage to attack prone creatures).
  • Wolves have Keen Hearing and Smell — impossible to sneak up on downwind. Advantage on Perception checks involving smell or hearing.
  • The northern pack near mile 4 is more aggressive than usual lately — same territory disruption driving the [[displacer_beast|displacer beast]] movement from the High Forest.
