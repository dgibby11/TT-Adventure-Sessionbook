Overview

Displacer Beasts are large, six-limbed feline predators with tentacles protruding from their shoulders and a magical ability to appear to be somewhere they are not. They are native to the [[high_forest|High Forest]] but rarely venture near its edge — historically, they have sufficient prey in the interior and no reason to risk contact with the campus perimeter.

Sightings near the forest edge have increased significantly over the past two terms. Three confirmed sightings, two probable. The [[expedition_club|Expedition Volunteers]] have logged all five and noted the pattern without explaining it. [[prof_thalia_varn|Professor Varn]] has been informed.

Displacement Ability

A displacer beast's most dangerous feature is that you are almost certainly not looking at where it actually is. Its magical displacement means attack rolls have disadvantage unless the attacker has True Sight or similar capabilities. The beast itself does not suffer equivalent confusion — it knows exactly where you are.

Ranged attacks are at the same disadvantage. Area effects (spells that cover a zone, not a target) work normally. This is useful to know before engaging.

Combat Profile

  • Displacement: Attack rolls at disadvantage until the beast takes damage (then suppressed until its next turn)
  • Tentacle attacks: Two per turn, 7 ft reach, 2d6+3 bludgeoning — can knock a Medium creature off its feet
  • Pounce: If it moves 20+ ft and hits with a claw, target must make DC 13 STR save or fall prone, and the beast gets a bonus bite attack
  • AC 13, HP 85, Speed 40 ft

DM Only

The increased edge sightings are driven by disruption deep in the [[high_forest|High Forest]] — the same disruption the wood elves are watching. Something has destabilised the interior ecosystem. The displacer beasts are being pushed out of their territory, not drawn to the campus. If PCs investigate, they'll find the beasts are moving away from something, not toward anything.

A lone displacer beast is a moderate threat for the party (CR 3 vs. Level 5 = easy). A mated pair is a serious encounter. If the source of the interior disruption is eventually investigated, the deep forest contains considerably worse.
