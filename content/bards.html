Location
East-central campus; acoustically distinct from fifty feet away
Focus
Performance magic, enchantment, inspiration, illusion, social manipulation
Known for
Best parties; most dramatic interpersonal incidents; suspiciously effective rumor generation

Facilities

A converted amphitheatre with practice rooms, a rehearsal hall, a small performance stage, and acoustic properties that suggest the building was designed by someone who understood sound at a level that exceeds conventional architecture. Sound redirects in ways that shouldn't be structurally possible. Three rooms have spots where a whispered sentence can be heard clearly on the opposite side of the space. Students know where they are. Faculty pretend not to.

Flowering vines cover the main archway and are maintained with a care that is conspicuously out of step with the rest of the department's relationship with upkeep. No one has ever been observed tending them, but they are always healthy.

Culture

The Bards Department operates at a permanent elevated emotional pitch that its students consider normal and that everyone else finds exhausting. Disagreements are theatrical. Triumphs are celebrated at volume. Personal crises are, by convention, handled publicly and with a certain performed dignity that is still, technically, public.

The department is also, quietly, one of the most strategically sophisticated programs at the Academy. The performance curriculum is the visible surface of an education in persuasion, social intelligence, and information management that faculty describe as "communication theory" and that graduates describe as "learning how rooms work." Students who pay close attention discover this by the end of the first year. Students who don't pay close attention are, themselves, being practiced on.

The [[dueling_society|Arcane Dueling Society]] Connection

Bards students are disproportionately represented in the Dueling Society's match promotion and commentary roles. They do not often compete in matches. They are very often in the room when significant conversations happen afterward.
