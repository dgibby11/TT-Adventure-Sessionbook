Location
Northeast campus, elevated terrain — the tower is visible from most of campus
Focus
Arcane magic, transmutation, magical theory, research methodology
Notable faculty
[[prof_aldous_fenwick|Professor Aldous Fenwick]] (tenured, arcane theory); [[oswald_crumb|Professor Oswald Crumb]] (arcane studies, field research)
Known for
The tower's top floor; the smell; something going wrong on a regular schedule

Facilities

A tower complex — the primary spire reaches seven stories. Lower floors contain classrooms, a reference library with restricted sections clearly marked and a less-clearly-marked additional restricted alcove on the second floor, and faculty offices. Upper floors are research laboratories with restricted access. The top floor has no windows, no listed official purpose in any campus document, and produces a faint burning smell that arrives in the administrative wing on certain mornings and is noted in no incident reports.

Culture

The Wizards Department attracts students with a high tolerance for precise language and a low tolerance for imprecision in others. The culture is competitive in a way that is mostly professional and occasionally personal — the distinction depends on the year's cohort and which professor is running first-year theory.

[[prof_aldous_fenwick|Fenwick]] is tenured and cannot be removed. This is known campus-wide. His students know it. He knows they know it. He has incorporated it into his pedagogy in a way that is either brilliant or insufferable, depending on which of his graduates you ask — and his graduates have strong opinions, which is perhaps the point.

[[oswald_crumb|Crumb]] runs a more collegial section of the tower and has significantly better relationships with students, which is less reassuring than it sounds once you know more about his external commitments.

The Tower's Plants

Something is growing on the exterior wall of the upper tower, seven stories up, in full shade. No soil. No obvious attachment point. The plants appear healthy and are visibly, slowly spreading. No official explanation has been offered. No faculty member who has been asked about it has continued the conversation.
