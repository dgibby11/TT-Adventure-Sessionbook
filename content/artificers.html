Facilities

A maze of workshops, forges, and cluttered laboratory rooms connected by passageways that have accumulated so many project components, failed prototypes, and reference materials that navigation requires familiarity rather than maps. The department has twice requested a larger building. Both times, the Academic Council noted that the department's current use of space would fill any building they were given within a term.

The forge at the rear of the main workshop is original construction — dating to the Academy's earliest years, before the Artificers Department formally existed as a program. What it was used for then is not in the official record.

Culture

The Artificers Department attracts students who define success as "did it work" and only secondarily "did it work without catching fire." This produces extraordinary results and occasional facility damage. The department's relationship with campus maintenance is ongoing, mutual, and at times adversarial.

Students here collaborate freely across projects in a way that other departments find unusual. The concept of proprietary methodology does not take hold easily in a room where everyone can see each other's work and where asking questions is how you avoid a structural failure. Credit assignment is handled loosely and litigated heavily at the end of every term.

External Supplies

The department sources materials through approved Academy channels and, unofficially, through whatever students can acquire independently. A locked cabinet in the northeast workshop bears a supplier mark from the [[thornwick_consortium|Thornwick Arcane Consortium]]. No current faculty have explained its presence.
