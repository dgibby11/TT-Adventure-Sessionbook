Overview

Tavin Crestfall is not technically visible to students outside the Rogues Department, but enough people know the name that he qualifies as a public figure by reputation if not by presence. He is a fourth-year student with a fourth-year student's apparent concern for his academic standing and a considerably more complex set of actual concerns running alongside it.

Public Profile

He is known — by name, by reputation — as someone who can solve problems. What problems, how, at what cost, are details that circulate informally and shift depending on who is telling the story. No one has caught him at anything specific. [[master_veris|Master Veris]] knows his name. Veris does not know his role in the [[dead_hours|Dead Hours]] operation, or has chosen not to know it. The distinction, in practice, is the same.

The Mine

He spends time in the deeper sections of the [[rogues|Rogues mine]] that most students don't reach. He knows the navigation there better than anyone currently enrolled. He has a field journal documenting routes, timings, and observations. The journal is currently in the [[rogues_deep_shafts|deep shafts]] — left there, not lost. Whether that is a meaningful distinction is unclear.

DM Only

Crestfall runs the logistics of the Dead Hours — scheduling, security, credit management. The operation functions partly because he is very good at it and partly because faculty are choosing not to look. He is aware of both factors and plans accordingly.

The journal was left in the deep shafts because the last entry is mid-sentence and he went back for it and couldn't find it. He went back three times. He hasn't told anyone the journal is missing because explaining what's in it is worse than leaving it unfound. The [[crestfall_journal|journal]] is out there. What happened when he went back and couldn't locate it is something he's been trying not to think about too precisely.

He refers to something in the shafts as "the permanent tenant." He does not know what it is. He knows where it is, approximately, and has been mapping around it rather than toward it. He considers this a reasonable approach that he has been maintaining for two semesters.
