Overview

Fennick Mosswood has been Head Librarian for nineteen years. He has memorized the location of every book in the unrestricted collection — floor, shelf, position — and is accurate to within two places in either direction for books that have been recently disturbed. He can tell you the acquisition date, condition, and most recent borrower of any volume in the main collection. He is precise, consistent, and entirely comfortable being interrupted.

The Restricted Floor

The restricted floor is not his jurisdiction. He says this with no particular emphasis, every time it comes up. If pressed on what "jurisdiction" means in this context, he explains that the restricted collection has a separate administrative authority — the Academic Council, historically, though the practical management has been unclear since Year 47 — and that his role as librarian covers the main and reference collections only. He is not evasive about this. He is specific. The specificity is more unsettling than evasion would be.

Relationships

He has a collegial relationship with [[prof_aldous_fenwick|Professor Fenwick]], which has been noted by several students as an unlikely pairing. Neither of them has commented on it. The relationship predates Fenwick's current tenure at the Academy and seems to involve some shared history that neither has explained.

DM Only

Fennick knows the [[restricted_floor|restricted floor]] exists and has never entered it. He knows the contents were catalogued only through Year 47. He knows the iron door has a monitoring ward. He knows all of this because he installed the monitoring ward himself, at the request of an administrator who has since left the Academy, and has never seen the log of what it has recorded.

He was told the ward data went to the Academic Council. He has never verified this. He's been managing low-level unease about this for seventeen years by focusing on the parts of his job he can fully account for, which is most of it. If players ask him the right question — specifically, who has access to the monitoring log — he will have to admit he doesn't know, and that admission will visibly cost him something.
