Overview

Prof. Ysera runs the Sorcerers Department with a combination of genuine competence and compulsive documentation. She has kept a written log of every wild magic surge she has experienced in the last decade, including date, time, casting context, emotional state, proximity to enchanted objects, weather, and twelve other variables she has identified as potentially correlating. There is no pattern. She continues logging.

Teaching

She teaches instinct management — how to work with wild magic rather than suppress it, and how to remain functional when it behaves unpredictably, which it always eventually does. She is exceptionally patient with students who surge unexpectedly in class. She has a prepared response for each type of surge. Some of these responses are more prepared than others. The ones involving large-scale fire effects are very well-prepared.

Personality

She is methodical, observant, and slightly optimistic in a way that has never been rewarded. She continues to believe the surge pattern will emerge if she gathers enough data. Faculty from other departments find this approach to an inherently unpredictable phenomenon either admirable or exhausting, depending on the faculty member. She is aware of both assessments and continues logging.

DM Only

The surges have increased in frequency near the [[fail_chamber|FAIL Chamber]] and near the eastern end of the main building. She has noted this in the log under "proximity to enchanted infrastructure" without yet drawing a conclusion she's willing to act on. The log, if the players can access it, is an extremely accurate map of where the underground enchantment is most active, because surges respond to ambient magical density and she has been documenting location consistently for a decade.

She does not know this is what the log documents. She is looking for a different pattern. The pattern she has not looked for is visible in the data she has already collected.
