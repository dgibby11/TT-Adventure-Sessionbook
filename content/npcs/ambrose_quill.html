Overview

Ambrose Quill has worked in the Main Complex for twenty-seven years. He was the Administrative Secretary under the previous headmistress and has remained in post through the transition. He knows where every file is, who had access to it, and what state it was in before and after. He is more knowledgeable about the administrative history of the Academy than anyone currently working in it, including [[headmistress_dowe|Headmistress Dowe]].

Role

He manages schedules, correspondence, filing, and the movement of documents between offices. He knows what has been sent to the unnamed door on the administrative corridor. He has never tried to find out what that door leads to. He processes the incoming mail, routes it to the correct recipients, and treats the no-name door as a destination like any other. This has been his approach for twenty-seven years and he has not changed it.

Manner

Quietly pleasant. Helpful in the specific sense of providing exactly what was asked for, accurately, without elaboration. Students who visit the outer office find him efficient and not unkind. Faculty find him indispensable. He is the institutional memory that keeps the complex functional when administrative priorities shift, which they have done several times during his tenure.

DM Only

Quill knows more than he has ever processed consciously. Twenty-seven years of routing documents, managing access logs, and watching the administrative patterns of three headmistresses means he has been present for most of what has happened in the Main Complex — not involved, not a witness in the legal sense, but present and attentive in the way of someone whose job requires them to notice everything and keep most of it to themselves.

He has not tried to find out what the unnamed door leads to because he has worked out, over two decades of observation, that the people who tried to find out what it led to did not continue working in the complex afterward. He does not know whether this correlation is causal. He considers it sufficiently suggestive to act on. He will confirm the door exists if asked. He will confirm it receives deliveries. He will not say more without either significant trust or evidence that not saying more is now the more dangerous choice.
