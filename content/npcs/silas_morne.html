Appearance

Silas Morne is a thin man with silver hair combed precisely to one side, ink-stained fingers, and a posture that suggests he has been sitting at a counter for thirty years — because he has. He wears a grey vest over a white shirt with the sleeves rolled exactly to the elbow. He smells faintly of parchment and cedar wood. His reading spectacles are always on his head, never on his nose, because he hasn't needed them to read for twelve years but he would feel wrong without them.

Personality

Silas is polite, precise, and faintly amused by most things. He does not lecture students on the wisdom of their purchases, but he will let a long pause do the work for him. He has a deep institutional loyalty to the Academy that coexists, uncomfortably, with a growing private suspicion that the Academy is not what it presents itself to be.

He treats the credit ledger as a sacred document. He has never once given anyone a discount, not even himself on the staff rate — which, he notes without bitterness, barely covers the ink he uses to maintain their records.

DM Only — The Private Ledger

For the past nine years, Silas has been quietly keeping a second ledger — unofficial, stored inside the false bottom of his desk drawer — cataloguing purchase anomalies he cannot explain.

  • Students purchasing large quantities of rope and torches the night before "cancelled" field exercises that were later classified
  • Faculty purchases of unusual reagents billed to operational accounts that Silas cannot find in the official budget
  • Three students who made large purchases in the final weeks of their enrolment and were never seen graduating
  • A recurring order for twelve units of a consumable listed only as "Specimen Containment — Biological" that Silas has never seen delivered to any listed lab

Silas does not know what to do with any of this. He is sixty-two years old, owns no weapons, and has no powerful friends. He has not spoken of it to anyone. If the PCs demonstrate that they are trustworthy — and ideally, alive — he may eventually show them the second ledger.

Using Silas as an ally: He can tell the party when someone has been buying things they shouldn't. He cannot leave the store, will not testify formally, and will deny everything if directly confronted by faculty. But he can "lose" a document or "forget" to log a purchase if he believes it serves the right ends.
