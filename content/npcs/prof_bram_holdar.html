Overview

Prof. Bram Holdar is a broad-shouldered half-orc in his mid-fifties who spent two decades as a working adventurer before anyone convinced him to teach. He has not stopped being an adventurer. He has merely changed the context in which he practices it.

He runs the Barbarians Department around a single conviction: theory is the enemy of instinct, and instinct is the only thing that keeps you alive. The curriculum reflects this. There are no written exams. There are no study guides. There are performances — timed, observed, and graded on whether the student made a decision and committed to it, regardless of outcome.

Personality

Holdar expresses opinions as challenges rather than statements. He does not say "that approach is wrong." He says "prove it works." Students who take him up on this generally find he has no objection to being wrong, provided you are right convincingly. Students who don't take him up on it find the semester very long.

He has a specific contempt for hesitation. Less for cowardice — cowardice can be trained out. Hesitation, he believes, is a failure of commitment, and commitment is the one thing he teaches that can't be broken down into steps.

History

His pre-Academy career is not something he discusses in structured terms, but students who pay attention have pieced together: wilderness campaigns in the Nether Mountains, something involving a sea voyage that went significantly wrong, and at least one incident that resulted in scars he explains only as "a disagreement I won." He arrived at the Academy already known. To whom, for what, he hasn't said.

DM Only

Holdar has had two students go through the [[fail_chamber|FAIL Chamber]] and come back changed in ways he did not expect and cannot characterize. He doesn't know what the Chamber does. He knows what it produces, and what it produced in those two cases was not what he sent in. He hasn't sent anyone else in since Year 54 without thinking about it twice.

He is not part of the [[secret_society|Secret Society]] and would not join if asked. He distrusts anything that requires oaths, symbols, or secrecy for its own sake. He trusts what he can physically test. The Chamber is the one thing on campus he can't test, and that bothers him in a way he doesn't know how to resolve.
