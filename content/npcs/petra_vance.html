Overview

Petra Vance noticed the sealed chapels on day one. She asked about them during her first week. She received the standard response — "not current curriculum" — and asked again, more specifically, at the end of the first month. She received the same response. She has been asking variations of the same question for a year and a half, each time more precisely framed than the last, each time receiving a non-answer delivered with decreasing patience.

Current Approach

She has stopped asking. This is recent. The shift happened three months ago when she determined that asking was not producing information and that the absence of information was itself information she could work with. She has been researching instead — [[old_library|library]] records, historical texts, the academic publications of [[archcleric_senna|Archcleric Senna]] from before her appointment — looking for what the sealed chapels were consecrated to before they were sealed.

Manner

She is patient, methodical, and has very good manners that she has not allowed to become a reason to stop. Other students in the Clerics Department have noticed the shift in her approach. A few have asked what she's found. She tells them she's still researching. This is accurate, though perhaps incomplete.

DM Only

Petra has found the names of two of the three deities whose chapels were sealed. Both names appear in texts that the library does not flag as restricted — they are obscure, not forbidden, and she has been careful enough in her approach to stay within the unrestricted collection. She knows the names. She does not yet know what the names mean, or what the third deity is, or why all three chapels were sealed rather than just one.

She is the most dangerous kind of investigator: patient, careful, and not alarmed by the absence of easy answers. If the players engage with her, she is a genuine source of documented information about the sealing. She is also a liability if whoever sealed the chapels decides that a second-year student with a very good research methodology has gotten too close. She has not yet gotten too close. She is getting there methodically.
