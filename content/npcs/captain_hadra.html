Overview

Captain Hadra runs the campus security rotation with the efficiency of someone who knows exactly what her authority covers and exactly where it stops. The perimeter is secure. The grounds are monitored. The main complex is controlled. The department buildings are not her problem, formally, because each department maintains its own access protocols and the administration has not changed this arrangement in the eleven years she has been in post.

Manner

She is professional in the specific way of someone who has decided that professionalism is the only sustainable approach to a situation that would otherwise require constant frustration. She manages the guards, the patrol schedule, and the incident log without complaint. She has filed one formal complaint about her jurisdictional limitations in eleven years. It was not acknowledged. She is aware it was not acknowledged. She has not filed another complaint, which is not the same as being satisfied with the outcome.

The Incident Log

The security incident log is meticulous. It is also incomplete, because anything that happens inside a department building does not get reported to her office and she cannot compel it. The [[contraband_log|contraband log]] is hers; she maintains it personally. Three entries in the last two years have been redacted above her authority level, which she noticed and documented in a separate file.

DM Only

Hadra is the cleanest institutional actor on campus. She is not involved in anything the players are investigating, she is not protecting anyone she shouldn't be, and she is frustrated by the limits of her position in exactly the way a competent professional should be. She is the most straightforwardly trustworthy person in the Main Complex, which makes her an asset and also makes her a threat to whoever is managing the three redacted contraband entries.

If players bring her something she can act on within her authority, she will act on it. If they bring her something outside her authority, she will tell them exactly who they need to go to and what documentation they need to bring. This is more useful than it sounds. She also knows things she's observed that she has not been able to file reports about, and she keeps those observations in her head. A player who asks her what she's seen — not what she's reported, what she's seen — will get different answers.
