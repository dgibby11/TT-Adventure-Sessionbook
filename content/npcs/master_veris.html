Overview

Master Veris arrived at the Academy in Year 31 with adequate credentials, a letter of appointment from an administrator who has since retired, and no background that anyone has been able to verify from before Year 28. He describes himself as human. Students have discussed this. Nobody has formally challenged it.

The Face Problem

Students are not certain they have seen Master Veris's face the same way twice. This is documented in the informal record — enough separate people have made note of it that it qualifies as a pattern rather than coincidence. His explanation is that the lighting in the [[rogues|mine passages]] varies significantly and produces optical effects. This explanation is plausible and probably false.

Teaching

He runs the department with precise structural looseness — there is a curriculum, it has objectives, but how those objectives are reached is left deliberately open. Students who arrive expecting instruction in specific techniques find instead that they are being assessed on what they already know. The techniques they lack become apparent to them during that process, at which point instruction is available but not provided unprompted.

He is aware of [[tavin_crestfall|Crestfall]]'s name and role in the Academy. He is not aware of his specific operations in the [[dead_hours|Dead Hours]], in the way that a person can choose not to know something that would oblige them to act on it.

DM Only

Veris's face problem is real. He is not using mundane disguise — the optical inconsistency is more specific than that. What he is, and what Year 28–30 contains in his history, is intentionally left open. Possible explanations: minor transmutation that has become habitual; a magical affliction he has managed for decades; something more structural. He has not confided in anyone at the Academy. He has not been asked in a way he couldn't deflect. Players who press carefully and correctly will find he does not actually claim to be the same person they saw last time — he changes the subject instead.
