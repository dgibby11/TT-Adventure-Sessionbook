Overview

Thessa Rann is at the top of her fourth-year cohort by every metric the Fighters Department tracks: sparring record, weapons proficiency assessments, tactical examinations, and the informal metric of who the other students look at when something goes wrong and a decision needs to be made. She is on the [[dueling_society|Dueling Society]] roster and has won more formal bouts this year than any student in the current bracket.

Public Record

[[prof_thalia_varn|Professor Varn]] has recommended her for two post-graduation appointments — both are good positions at institutions that would value her record. The letters of recommendation are accurate and generous. Varn considers her among the best students she has taught in the past decade. This assessment is correct. What Varn does not know is that Rann has no intention of accepting either appointment.

Manner

She is composed, direct, and writes letters home every week without fail. The letters home are one of those details about her that other students find slightly incongruous — not because it's unusual to miss one's family, but because the discipline with which she maintains it suggests it serves a function beyond sentiment. She does not discuss the third option she is planning. She has not told Varn, which is a decision she has made and revisited approximately once a week since Year 3.

DM Only

The third option is a position she has arranged herself, outside the Academy's recommendation network. It is not with a known institution. It is with a small, private operation that has been recruiting selectively from graduating classes across the city for three years. She has done enough due diligence to know it's not illegal. She has not done enough due diligence to be certain of everything else. The letters home include coded updates to her family about the plan — she is more careful than she appears, and she has been planning this since Year 2.

She is not in any danger the players need to rescue her from. She is a player in her own story, which may intersect with theirs depending on what the private operation turns out to be.
