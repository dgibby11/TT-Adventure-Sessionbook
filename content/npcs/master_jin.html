Overview

Master Jin is a dragonborn who appears to be in his eighties and moves like someone who has decided aging is a suggestion rather than a rule. He has been at the Academy for an undisclosed period that faculty records place at "at least thirty years" and students place at "forever." Both estimates may be approximately correct.

Teaching Method

He teaches by not teaching. Students are assigned tasks without explanation — carry this to the east courtyard, hold this position until I return, spend an hour not moving — and are expected to complete them without asking why. Students who figure out why they were assigned a specific task are considered to have learned something. Students who complete the task correctly but never figure out why are considered to have learned something smaller. Students who ask why before starting are reassigned.

What he is actually teaching is attention — not to instruction, but to the work itself and what it reveals. The tasks are diagnostic. His silence during them is deliberate. Faculty from other departments find this pedagogy baffling. Students who survive the first term tend to defend it fiercely and with some difficulty explaining why.

Manner

He does not raise his voice. He does not repeat himself. He answers questions with questions often enough that students stop asking questions they haven't already answered themselves. The one time a student successfully argued with him on a point of technique, he bowed slightly and changed the assignment for the following week. He did not acknowledge the argument. The changed assignment was the acknowledgment.

DM Only

Master Jin has been aware of what is beneath the campus for longer than most current faculty have been alive. He has neither acted on this knowledge nor pretended not to have it. The [[fail_chamber|FAIL Chamber]] is the one space on campus he declines to enter, and his explanation — that the space "asks the wrong question" — is the most he has said on the subject. He has never elaborated. Students who have pressed him on it received a new task the following day. It is not clear whether the new task was related. It is not clear it wasn't.
