Overview

Reva Kettner has run the Food Court kitchens for twenty-five years. The operation produces three meals a day for approximately three hundred students and forty faculty on a schedule that has never, in a quarter century, been more than fifteen minutes late. She attributes this to planning. The planning is genuinely impressive. The kitchen is also genuinely impressive — small, efficient, and staffed by people she has personally trained to her specifications.

Personality

She is practical and has no patience for anything that is not practical. She manages [[lenny_goldvein|Goldvein's]] provisioning budget with the kind of efficiency that comes from twenty-five years of knowing exactly how much latitude she has and how far she can push it. She pushes it regularly. Goldvein has never caught her at it, because she is more familiar with the budget than he is.

The Service Hatch

She has a key to the service hatch that leads to the underground lift system. She has used it exactly once, six years ago, for reasons she will not discuss. Three days after that visit, the floor near the hatch started running consistently warmer than the rest of the room. The maintenance log shows four reports filed about this. Zero responses. She has not filed another report. She has also not gone back down.

DM Only

Reva went down the hatch six years ago because she had reason to believe something in the [[underground_storage|underground storage]] was affecting the kitchen inventory — specific items going missing in a pattern she couldn't account for. What she found was not missing inventory. She doesn't describe what she found, to anyone, at all. She came back up, locked the hatch, and has had [[lenny_goldvein|Goldvein]] order replacement supplies through a different channel ever since without explaining why.

She knows something is wrong below the Food Court. She has made a decision — which she has not articulated but which her behavior clearly reflects — that it is not her problem to solve and that the wisest course is to be as normal as possible and wait to see who else notices. She is watching to see if anyone notices. She has not yet decided what she'll do when someone does.
