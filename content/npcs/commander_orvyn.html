Overview

Commander Orvyn runs the Paladins Department as a command structure, not a school. There are ranks. There are expectations that attach to ranks. There are consequences for failing to meet those expectations that are graduated and clearly documented in the department handbook, which Orvyn wrote, revised, and has revised again twice since. The revisions are tracked. Students are expected to know which version is current.

Personality

He is imposing in full ceremonial plate, which he wears during instruction and sees no reason not to. He has a precise opinion on the correct wording of every major vow in the paladin tradition, and that opinion changes when new evidence warrants it. He does not announce these changes. He updates the department materials, and students who were relying on the previous version find out at assessment. Several have taken this personally. He considers that a misallocation of feeling.

He disagrees with [[archcleric_senna|Archcleric Senna]] on how much students should know about the practical limits of divine channeling. He has filed the disagreement formally. The file exists. Nothing has come of it. He is prepared to file again.

Relationships

He coordinates with the [[clerics|Clerics Department]] on matters of divine practice and finds the relationship professionally essential and personally difficult. He visits the [[memorial_garden|Memorial Garden]] on the first of each month to update what he describes as "outstanding obligations." What this means is not fully clear to anyone except, presumably, him.

DM Only

Orvyn's Oath of Glory is sincere and his record is clean. He is not involved in anything the players will be investigating. He is, however, the person who will be most genuinely useful to them if they need institutional leverage and can demonstrate a legitimate grievance — because his response to a documented wrong is to file the appropriate action and then file it again until it is addressed. This is an unusual property in a faculty member. Players who figure this out have an asset.

The monthly Garden visits are exactly what they appear to be: former students and colleagues he considers his responsibility to remember. He has not said this to anyone. He also hasn't hidden it.
