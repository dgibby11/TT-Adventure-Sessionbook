Overview

Archcleric Senna runs the Clerics Department with the kind of authority that does not need to announce itself. She is present in the department more than she is in her office; she is in her office more than she is anywhere else; she is nowhere without knowing exactly what is happening there.

Teaching

She teaches doctrine, ritual structure, and divine channeling theory with complete precision and no visible affect. Students who come to her with theological questions find she has an answer. Students who come to her with questions about the sealed chapels find the conversation ends with the same phrase it always ends with: "That is not current curriculum." The phrasing has not changed in twenty years. Neither has the tone.

Relationships

She knows [[headmistress_dowe|Headmistress Dowe]] well enough to finish her sentences, which she has done exactly once in front of other people and clearly regretted. She attends every [[memorial_garden|Garden]] memorial without exception. She has never given a speech at one — she stands at the edge, observes, and leaves when it's over.

Her relationship with [[commander_orvyn|Commander Orvyn]] is formally cordial and substantively guarded. They overlap on matters of divine practice. They do not agree on how much students should be told about what divine practice actually does.

DM Only

Senna knows why the three chapels were sealed. She was present when the decision was made. The short version: the deities those chapels were consecrated to had, across three separate incidents over twelve years, responded to petitions from within those rooms in ways that were not consistent with the divine tradition those petitions invoked. Something else was answering. The faculty of that era chose to seal the rooms rather than investigate further. Senna agreed. She still does.

The [[archival_prayer_text|prayer text]] in the unsealed chapel is the one she's most concerned about. She has translated enough of it to know what it was designed to invoke, and the entity it describes is not in any divine taxonomy the Clerics Department teaches. She has not removed it because she believes it is safer to know where it is.

As an ally: She will not volunteer information about the chapels. If players have specific, accurate knowledge and ask the right question, she may confirm rather than deny. She won't lie about what she knows, but she will decline to discuss it until the question makes that stance untenable.
