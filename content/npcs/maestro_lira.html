Overview

Maestro Lira has been running the Bards Department for twenty-three years, long enough that students arriving now encounter a department that is entirely her design. Before that she was a working performer, court musician, and something else that her professional biography does not describe specifically. The gap in the record is approximately eight years and the subject does not come up.

Teaching

She teaches performance, composition, and the theory of resonant enchantment with what students describe as "terrifying precision." She can hear a pitch error from the far end of the [[bards_stage|performance stage]] and identify which student made it without turning around. Students have tested this. The tests have not been successful.

She knows about the resonance enchantment in the building. She has never explained it to students. When asked, she describes the acoustic behavior as "the room doing what rooms do when they've been treated correctly," which is not an explanation and which she knows is not an explanation.

Habits

She plays three instruments simultaneously when she thinks no one is listening. Three confirmed students have heard her do it. Two of them tried to describe what they heard and couldn't agree on what the third instrument was. She has not been asked about it directly. It is unclear whether she would answer if she were.

She appears regularly at the [[food_court|Food Court]] at off-peak hours, eating alone, and has been observed there in conversation with [[dueling_society|Dueling Society]] students in a way that does not look like a faculty-student interaction.

DM Only

The eight-year gap in Lira's record was spent studying under a figure associated with the founding era of the Academy — not one of the three founders, but someone adjacent to the founding project who was responsible for certain enchantments in the original building. She knows significantly more about what the resonance system was built to do than anyone currently on faculty. She chose to take the department position because she wanted to be near it, not to teach.

The [[resonance_tuning_fork|tuning fork]] she keeps in her office is not a standard instrument. She knows exactly what it does. She has not used it in eleven years. The question of why she stopped is a useful hook: what happened eleven years ago, and what did she hear when she used it?
