Location
North-central campus; the boundary between "the Druids area" and "everywhere else" is genuinely unclear
Focus
Nature magic, wildshape, druidic tradition, primal forces, moon cycles
Known for
The best weather on campus; uninvited plant growth in adjacent departments; the midnight activity schedule that no one outside the department entirely understands

Facilities

A cluster of structures that seem to have grown rather than been built. Stone-and-timber halls with living root systems integrated into the walls — not decoratively, structurally. The department stopped distinguishing between the building and the ecosystem around it sometime in its third decade of operation. Whatever is in the walls is load-bearing. Maintenance staff check with the Druids Department before doing anything to the building. The Druids Department sometimes says no.

The interior courtyard has not been landscaped since Year 11. What grows there grows there because it chose to. The department will tell you which plants are safe to touch. This is a curriculum offering, not a courtesy.

The Weather

The immediate vicinity of the Druids Department has measurably better weather than the surrounding campus — milder winters, less standing water in spring, slightly higher sunlight duration. Students claim they're making it happen. Faculty have never confirmed or denied this. The administrative wing has asked three times about whether it would be possible to extend the effect campus-wide. The faculty head has each time responded with a letter that technically answers the question and does not say yes.

Culture

The Druids Department operates on a schedule that follows natural rhythms rather than the Academy calendar. Peak activity at dawn, at dusk, and during the midnight moon work. The department's mid-afternoon quiet is genuine — students rest, plants are monitored, and nothing is demanded.

New students are often surprised by the rigor. "Nature magic" suggests something loose and intuitive. The Druids curriculum is precise, patient, and cumulative in a way that punishes anyone who tries to skip a step. The natural world is exact. The students are expected to become so.
