Overview

The deepest chamber of the underground storage system has a floor that was cleared and relaid in stone cut to a geometric pattern. The pattern is large enough to fill the chamber floor — approximately thirty feet across — and consists of interlocking angular sections that converge on a central point. The stone is set cleanly. The mortar is old enough to have settled. The pattern is new enough to have been laid during the Academy's founding era.

The Space

The chamber is otherwise unremarkable — stone walls, low ceiling, a passage entry from the main storage corridor. The air is cooler here than in the upper levels and carries a faint smell that is not mineral and not decay and has not been successfully characterized by anyone who has been down here. The [[bodak|Bodak]] circles the perimeter of this room. Not the adjacent corridors. This room specifically.

Current State

Whatever the pattern was designed to activate requires components that are not currently present. The pattern itself is intact. The activation state is dormant. This condition has been stable for decades. Whether the stability is passive — the components simply absent — or actively maintained is not clear from observation alone.

DM Only

The pattern was laid by the three founders during the Academy's establishment as part of the binding described in the [[founders_charter|Founders' Charter]]. Its purpose was to anchor the Academy's protective enchantments to a fixed physical point — a ritual anchor that would prevent certain kinds of interference with the building's magical infrastructure. The components required to activate it were distributed: one is in the [[sealed_urn|sealed urn]] in this chamber, one is in the East Annex sub-basement (the ninth compass point), and one was taken by [[brennan_ashcroft|Brennan Ashcroft]] when he left the Academy and has not been located since.

The Bodak circles this room because its death gaze is suppressed by the pattern's residual enchantment — not neutralized, but constrained. This was intentional. The founders anticipated needing a containment system that could be managed from outside the chamber. The Bodak was not the original containment approach, which no longer exists. The Bodak is an improvised solution that has held for longer than anyone expected it to.
