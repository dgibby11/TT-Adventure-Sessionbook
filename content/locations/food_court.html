Location
Central campus — the most reliably populated indoor space
Hours
Three meals per day; open between meals for general use
Overseen by
[[lenny_goldvein|Lenny Goldvein]] (budget and provisioning)

Overview

A wide, low-ceilinged hall with long communal tables that seat every department at once during peak meals — which is to say that the Fighters and Barbarians Departments, who arrive early and loud, have already claimed the center tables by the time anyone else gets there, and this arrangement has been stable for years. The kitchen alcoves along the south wall produce three meals on a schedule that every department has theoretically agreed to work around.

Between meals, the Food Court functions as the Academy's primary common ground: the place where students from all twelve departments occupy the same tables, argue across department lines, and conduct the informal social commerce that does not fit in any classroom. Study groups form here. Romances start here. Feuds are negotiated, if not resolved. The ambient sound level at midday meal is the loudest the campus gets — louder than the practice yards, because practice yards at least involve breaks.

Social Dynamics

Seating at the Food Court is entirely self-selected and rigidly conventional. Departments cluster. Within departments, cohort years cluster within that. The exception is a long table near the east windows that has, for as long as anyone currently enrolled can remember, been occupied by a shifting group of students from different departments who don't seem to have any obvious connection and who the social geography of the room has simply never absorbed into the usual pattern. No one knows why. No one has asked.

The Economy

The Food Court is the informal hub of [[academy_credits|Academy Credit]] circulation. Trades happen here — credits for services, credit for information, credit for items that technically belong to the supply system and have been redirected by someone who understands the supply system's soft points. [[lenny_goldvein|Goldvein]] is theoretically managing the credits budget. The [[dead_hours|Dead Hours]] underground economy runs partly on credits that entered the informal system through this room.

DM Only

The rear service hatch leads to the lift system for the [[underground_storage|Food Court Underground Storage]]. The hatch locks from below, which means someone underground can prevent access from above but not the reverse. The original design for this feature is not in the building plans.

The floor near the service hatch is consistently warmer than the rest of the room by a few degrees. This has been noted in maintenance reports four times. It has been addressed zero times. Goldvein's response to the most recent maintenance report was "structurally normal, no action required." He has not personally inspected the hatch area.

Social hook: The Food Court is where students talk. PCs who eat here and listen — not Perception checks, just listening — will hear the currents of student life: which professors are acting strangely, which areas of campus students are avoiding, which rumors have reached critical mass. Use this location as a passive information source.
