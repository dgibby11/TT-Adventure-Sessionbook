Overview

The main forge occupies the ground floor of the Artificers building and is the loudest space on campus during operation. It is almost always during operation. Metal fabrication, magical integration, heat treatment, and whatever [[tinkerer_dunn|Tinkerer Dunn's]] current project requires run on a schedule that senior students understand and that is posted on a board near the entrance in notation that first-year students describe as "suggestive" rather than informative.

The Space

Hot, loud, and organized in a way that rewards attention. The major pieces of equipment — the main forge, the cooling tanks, the integration bench where magical elements are added to fabricated pieces — are positioned around the room in a workflow that Dunn established and has not changed in eight years. Several items bolted to the floor have no clear purpose. When asked, Dunn says they are "legacy infrastructure." Students have stopped asking.

Testing

Items fabricated here are tested in a separate enclosed space off the southeast corner. The enclosed space has blast-resistant walls. It is used regularly. Three things have caught fire in the main forge this semester; both of the intentional ones are documented in the project logs. The unintentional one is also documented, in [[tinkerer_dunn|Dunn's]] handwriting, with the notation "investigate."

DM Only

The "legacy infrastructure" bolted to the floor is original to the building. It predates the Artificers Department's tenancy and appears in the original construction blueprints as "resonance dampeners — primary array." They are designed to suppress ambient magical resonance in the forge space, which would otherwise interfere with precise magical integration work. They are still functional. They are also why the [[alchemy_notebook|notebook]] left in the forge is harder to read with a magical detection than it should be — the dampeners reduce detection accuracy in this space significantly.
