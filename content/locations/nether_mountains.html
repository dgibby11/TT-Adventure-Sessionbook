Location
Nether Mountains — ~12 miles north of campus, across the [[river_rauvin|River Rauvin]]
Access
Overland via the north ferry crossing; 2-mile ascent from [[mountain_base|base camp]] to the expedition site
Elevation (dig site)
~7,000–9,000 ft — above the treeline, exposed granite terrain
Known resources
Crystallized bat guano (cave overhangs), petrified fungal spores (sedimentary layers), powdered elemental granite (above 8,000 ft), minor components throughout

[[oswald_crumb|Professor Oswald Crumb]] has led at least one student expedition to this site under the guise of academic component gathering. Two prior Academy expeditions also visited the site; both abandoned mid-season without filing completion reports.

Terrain

Above the [[mountain_base|foothills base camp]], the landscape opens entirely — no tree cover, no shelter except boulder formations and rock overhangs. The dig site occupies a south-facing granite ridge with exposed stone faces and loose scree fields. Previous seasons' dig frames still stand, partially collapsed. A stone equipment cache sits near the site, unlocked, empty.

Higher up the ridge: wind-carved stone pillars at the edge of a natural cliff face. A vein of dark mineral runs through the granite in lines too regular to be entirely natural — branching at consistent angles, running for approximately forty meters before disappearing into the rock. It does not appear in the Academy's survey documents.

Flora

Nothing grows above the dig site except lichen. The orange and black patches on the boulder faces are centuries old; the grey patches are actively growing, spreading a few centimeters per decade. In sheltered cracks at the dig site elevation: one stunted juniper, twisted to the east by decades of prevailing wind. That's all.

Fauna

Alpine choughs — black, crow-like birds with red legs — follow expedition groups at a distance. Scavengers that have learned expeditions mean dropped food. They are opportunistic and not shy. At altitude, golden eagles and a pair of falcons ride the thermals off the cliff face. Cave bats in the overhangs above the dig site emerge at dusk; the crystallized bat guano that Crumb collects comes from these colonies.

Mountain goats can be seen on distant ridges but do not approach. Their tracks cross the scree regularly. Occasionally something larger moves across the high snowfields — tracks only, no confirmed sighting in any expedition log.

Rock Formations

The ridge above the dig site has wind-carved pillars of stone that create narrow passages and false horizons. Sedimentary layers in the cliff face have been pushed near-vertical by ancient tectonic movement — the geology has been turned sideways, which is part of why the component deposits here are unusual. A natural bowl formation about three feet across sits in a flat section of exposed granite, perfectly smooth, with no natural explanation for the smoothness.

Evidence of Ancient Inhabitants

Cup marks carved into a flat outcropping near the dig site — rows of shallow round depressions, too deliberate to be natural. The weathering places them at centuries old, minimum. Someone made a faded sketch of them in the margin of the site log; no analysis was filed.

Foundation stones in a rough circle, mostly buried by scree, about thirty meters east of the dig site. The circle is roughly four meters in diameter — too small for habitation, the right size for a watchtower or a shrine. The stonework style does not match any Academy construction. A carved channel in the rock nearby directs snowmelt runoff in a specific direction — someone engineered it, and it still works.

DM Only

The true purpose of Crumb's expedition is a supply run for the [[thornwick_consortium|Thornwick Arcane Consortium]]. He has been using Academy field trips as cover for three years.

Blue dragon: A blue dragon has been reported in the cave systems to the north. This is why the mountain shakes faintly when tensions escalate at the dig site — the dragon is aware of activity on the ridge and is deciding whether it is interesting. Crumb knows about it. He assessed the risk as acceptable. He will admit this under pressure, just before strongly suggesting everyone leave immediately.

Earth Elemental: Triggered by excavation vibrations in the granite-rich zone above 8,000 ft. Documented in internal Consortium communications but not in any Academy record. The abjuration traces on the north dig face are a containment seal placed after a previous elemental emergence — something was disturbed and partially extracted before the team that placed the seal abandoned the site.

The dark mineral vein: Not a natural formation. It is a residual trace of something that was removed from this site — whatever was dug up and sealed at the north face left this as a byproduct. Crumb knows what it is and has been photographing it for the Consortium. He has no intention of explaining it to the party.

The ancient foundations: Pre-date the Academy by five centuries minimum. The cup marks are a ritual notation system — the same system appears, much smaller, on the underside of the founders' statue base at campus. The channel that still directs snowmelt is pointed at the dig site. Someone who lived here was using this location for something specific, and what they were doing involved water running over the exact spot where the excavation seal is now placed.
