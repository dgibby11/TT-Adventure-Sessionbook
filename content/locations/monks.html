Location
Eastern campus, separated from adjacent departments by an intentional buffer of open ground
Focus
Unarmed combat, Ki manipulation, mystical discipline, internal mastery
Known for
The only department where raising your voice is a disciplinary matter

Facilities

Spare and deliberate. Stone floors, paper screens dividing the practice areas, and a single enclosed meditation garden. The building is kept to a standard that the students maintain themselves — cleaning and maintenance are curriculum, not chores. Visitors who expect otherwise are corrected gently.

The meditation garden contains one cherry tree, tended with greater care than almost anything else on campus. Its origins are not discussed by faculty. It predates the current structure by a margin that no one has verified, because no one has been encouraged to look into it.

Culture

The Monks Department admits fewer students than any other program and turns away more applicants per seat. The selection criteria are not published. Students who receive offers are often surprised — they had not applied thinking they would be chosen.

Training here produces unusual results. The department has a higher rate of students developing abilities outside the standard curriculum — not wild or uncontrolled abilities, but extensions of expected forms that go further than the coursework accounts for. Faculty attribute this to the meditation practice. Some graduates attribute it to the garden.

The department maintains no formal graduation ceremony. Students who complete the program know when they have completed it. This has never required an administrative explanation.

The [[fail_chamber|FAIL Examination]]

Monks Department students consistently perform in the top cohort percentile on FAIL Chamber practical assessments, despite the department having no field-tactics curriculum. Dr. Voss has noted this in writing. The department head has not responded.
