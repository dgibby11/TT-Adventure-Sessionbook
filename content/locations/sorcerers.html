Location
Northeast campus, adjacent to the Wizards Department
Focus
Innate magical ability, bloodline traditions, wild magic management, instinctive casting
Known for
The ambient static; the lights that change color; having rebuilt the building three times

Facilities

Curved walls and high arched ceilings engineered by someone who had clearly thought carefully about what happens when unstable magical bloodlines manifest in an enclosed space with corners. The current building is the third version on this site. The first two are mentioned only in maintenance archives, and then only to note that "full facility replacement was required."

Students with measurably unstable bloodline signatures are issued proximity monitors: small resonance devices that chirp when their ambient magical output exceeds safe levels. This is presented as a safety measure. It also functions as an informal social sorting mechanism that the department has never publicly addressed.

Relationship with the Wizards Department

The proximity of the two arcane departments reflects something between collaboration and containment. Faculty relationships are professional. Student relationships vary — Wizards students who see magic as a learned discipline and Sorcerers students who carry it in their blood have a tension that is academic on the surface and existential underneath.

Both departments will tell you the other is necessary. Neither department will tell you without a slight pause.

Culture

The Sorcerers Department produces students with the highest ceiling and the most uneven floor of any program at the Academy. The ones who learn to work with their nature rather than against it tend to become extraordinary. The ones who can't reconcile the gap between what they're capable of and what they can control tend to transfer, or to stop attending without formally withdrawing — the department has a category in its records for this: "resolved departure."

The ambient lighting in the building responds to the emotional states of students nearby. This was not intentional. It has been present since the second rebuild. No one knows how to remove it, and several students have told the administration that they prefer it this way.
