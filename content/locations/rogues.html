Location
East of central campus; a disused iron mine in the cliff face along the eastern ridge
Focus
Stealth, precision, cunning, deception, lockpicking, information gathering
Known for
Students who are never where you expect them to be

Entry

The mine door has no handle. On the exterior face there is a small hole, roughly the diameter of a lockpick. Inserting a pick and executing the correct manipulation opens it. The mechanism changes on an undisclosed schedule. Students are expected to notice when it has changed and adapt without being told. There is no record of a non-student successfully picking the lock — though the department would not necessarily advertise that information if there were.

Facilities

The Rogues Department occupies a network of tunnels and chambers carved into the eastern ridge by iron miners who abandoned the site roughly a century before the Academy was founded. The entrance is not marked. The path to it is not maintained. Students in their first week spend an average of two days finding it before anyone tells them where it is — this is, in fact, a component of enrollment.

The mine itself has been adapted rather than renovated. Rough-cut stone passages of variable height and width; chambers that have served as ore sorting rooms, tool stores, and shaft-head operations and now serve as training spaces for those functions' modern equivalents. The deepest sections are not on any map given to first-year students. Students who reach them have either earned it or are in the wrong tunnel, which is also a known training outcome.

The location is not incidental. The ridge path is the least-monitored approach to campus. The mine has exits that are not entrances. Students who spend time here develop an extremely detailed understanding of where the campus watch does not go.

Culture

The Rogues Department does not publish a curriculum. Students who enroll receive a welcome document that describes the first term as "contextual skill orientation." The document is designed to be forgettable. Students who remember it discover that what it actually describes is an extended assessment of what each student already knows how to do, which is then used to tailor the second term to whatever gaps remain.

The department's relationship with the rest of the Academy is cordial and slightly wary in both directions. Faculty from other departments note that Rogues students always seem to arrive exactly when something interesting is happening. This is not coincidence. The Rogues Department teaches information gathering as a foundational skill, and the campus is a target-rich environment for anyone motivated to practice it.

The [[dead_hours|Dead Hours]]

The department's unofficial relationship with the Dead Hours underground gambling operation is a matter of knowing silence. Faculty are aware. The Dead Hours is, in practice, a capstone exercise in social engineering, probability management, and operating in an information-asymmetric environment. No faculty member has said this on the record. Several would say it off the record.
