Location
East of the Old Library; accessible from the main east path
Notable feature
Three memorial stones, one per founder; one empty tomb
Maintenance
Weekly; a Wednesday dusk access logged as "authorized grounds" — name unlisted

Physical Description

A formal garden enclosed by low stone walls with a central flagstone path leading to three memorial stones. The stones are tall, unadorned, and demonstrably older than everything that now surrounds them. White and silver flowers throughout — formally maintained. One rosebush along the north wall that no one planted, confirmed by records, present in every groundskeeping log going back to Year 6, is never removed.

Ambient sound doesn't carry into the garden the way proximity and campus noise would suggest it should. Students who study here note the quiet without being able to explain it. It is not the quiet of distance. It is the quiet of something absorbing sound before it arrives.

The Memorial Stones

Three stones, one for each named founder: Aldric Voss, Isolde Orath, Brennan Ashcroft. Each stone carries a name, dates, and a brief inscription. The inscriptions are different in style — they were not written by the same person. The Ashcroft stone's founding-year inscription is three years earlier than the official Academy founding date. This has never been addressed in any public document.

DM Only

The [[aldric_voss|Aldric Voss]] memorial conceals a tomb entrance beneath the stone. The tomb is empty — not robbed, not looted, never occupied. The slab shows no sign of having been moved after it was placed. Whatever was supposed to be interred here either never was, or was removed before the tomb was sealed.

The additional inscription at the base of Voss's stone, visible only with light and close examination, reads: "The work continues." In Voss's documented handwriting, per the archived correspondence in the [[old_library|Old Library]].

RESTRICTED: Do not surface the empty tomb or the base inscription until players actively investigate the garden at close range. The discovery should feel earned.

The abjuration pattern formed by the three stones is not protecting the garden. It is protecting something under the garden, and more specifically, something under the Voss memorial. What that something is has not yet been established in this campaign. The stones will resist divination attempts — not aggressively, but firmly, in the way that something old resists being examined rather than the way something recently warded does.
