Adjacent to Rangers Department, southwest campus — cave system at the forest edge
Resident
[[kass|Kass]] — adult owlbear, ~8ft long / 5ft tall standing, brown/rust coloring, scarred
Status
Contained

Physical Structure

A natural cave system, not a constructed facility. Multiple chambers: an outer entrance area (20ft wide, natural stone), a main holding chamber (~40ft deep, 25ft wide, ceilings up to 30ft), and a deeper rear chamber (a narrow passage leading to a natural exit deeper in the forest).

Security Layers

  • Iron Gate: 8ft tall, 6ft wide, heavy padlock (DC 16 Lockpicking / DC 18 Strength). Old; creaks loudly if opened roughly (Stealth DC 14).
  • Concealed Pit Trap: 10ft past the gate, 8ft deep, sharp rocks at bottom. DC 15 Perception to spot. 2d6 falling damage.
  • Magical Binding Circle: 30ft into the main chamber. DC 16 Arcana to identify. Deactivated by the phrase "Kess, you are free." If triggered accidentally, Kass becomes hostile.
  • Patrol Window: 5-minute gap between ranger patrols.

Main Chamber Features

  • Natural underground pool in the northwest corner (~10ft across, fed by a small spring)
  • Moss and fungi in patches throughout
  • Crevice in the ceiling (northwest, ~20ft up), 3–4ft wide, leads to the surface. Too narrow for Kass; usable by humanoids (DC 12 Athletics/Acrobatics).
  • Claw marks on the floor near the entrance — evidence of Kass's movement patterns

Kass — Behavioral Notes

HP
59
AC
13
Disposition
Docile, food-motivated, responds to Garrett Ashwood by name

Kass is normally non-aggressive and sleeps heavily — stirs at noise but does not immediately wake. Scarring on the flanks and face suggests a life in captivity and possibly pre-capture trauma. Will become hostile if the magical binding circle is triggered or if threatened.

DM Only

The owlbear line at this institution traces back to the founding era. [[maren_duskhollow|Maren Duskhollow]] — a figure associated with the original founders, though not one of the three principal architects — is believed to have brought the first owlbear ancestors to campus. This is not in official records but is informally maintained in Rangers Department lore.
