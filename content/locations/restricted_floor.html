Overview

The third floor of the Old Library is behind an iron door fitted with a monitoring ward. The catalogue covers the collection through Year 47. Everything acquired or moved after that date is unrecorded from the outside. No current maintenance staff enters. The administration lists the restricted floor as active; the practical management of it has been unclear since Year 47 when the librarian responsible for it retired and was not replaced.

The Space

The floor is organized as a normal library section — shelved volumes, reading tables, a card catalogue that stopped being updated. One section, in the northeast corner, runs ten degrees colder than the rest of the floor. The monitoring ward on the door logs approaches from both sides. It is unclear where those logs go, and [[fennick_mosswood|Fennick Mosswood]] does not have access to them.

Contents

The restricted collection covers subjects that are not part of current curriculum: advanced ritual theory, historical records of the campus's pre-Academy use, several texts in languages not taught here, and one section that the Year 47 catalogue simply labels "Founding Documents — Restricted." The Ninth Thesis source materials are in this section. Some of them have been replaced with blanks.

DM Only

The replacements happened in two separate incidents — one in Year 49, one in Year 53 — on both occasions when the monitoring ward logged an entry by someone using credentials that should not have existed. The logs go to a receiving terminal in the Main Complex that has not been staffed since Year 51. The logs are intact. They are accessible to anyone who finds the terminal, which is in the unnamed corridor off the administrative wing.

The northeast cold section is above the [[underground_storage|underground storage]] area where the temperature anomaly is concentrated. The texts in that section specifically cover what the cold means — they are the most accurate documentation of the ritual floor's purpose in the entire collection, and they are still here because whoever removed the other documents either didn't know what these were or decided they were too dangerous to move.
