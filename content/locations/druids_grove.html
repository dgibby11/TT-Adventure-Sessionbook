Location
Interior courtyard, [[druids|Druids Department]]
Access
Department students; open to the sky
Primary resident
One tree. Several dozen plants. Something else.

Overview

The inner courtyard of the Druids building is open to the sky and has been cultivated over several decades into something between a working garden and something that has been growing according to its own agenda. The central tree was here before the building. The building was constructed around it. The tree's face — two hollow knots and a long root-scar that could be read as a mouth — predates any living student's enrollment and was not carved. Students who work in the grove for more than a week stop mentioning the face.

The Plants

Several plants entered through the walls. This is not a metaphor — there are root systems that begin outside the building and continue through the stone into the grove, visible at the base of the courtyard walls where the mortar has been displaced. The roots are not causing structural damage. They appear to have found routes that avoided the load-bearing sections. At night, the root systems in the surrounding walls are measurably warmer than during the day.

Time in the Grove

[[archdruid_sevra|Archdruid Sevra]] spends more time here than in her office. Students with questions have learned to bring them here rather than to the office, and to be comfortable with silence as a response. The grove does not feel threatening. It feels attentive, which is a different quality that takes longer to notice.

DM Only

The [[grove_spirit|grove spirit]] is present in this space. It predates the Academy and was here when the Druids Department chose this location. It permitted the choice and has been in dialogue with Sevra for decades. It has opinions about certain students — those it redirects are students it has assessed as potentially significant, either as threats or as something more useful. The redirection is subtle: plants lean one direction, something underfoot gives slightly differently, the light changes. Players who notice the grove paying attention to them can try to engage with it directly, but it communicates in a vocabulary that requires Sevra to translate, and she will not translate everything it says.
