Location
Western campus, along the river wall
Focus
Martial combat, weapon mastery, tactical discipline
Known for
The loudest practice yard on campus; most broken training weapons per term
Head of Department
[[prof_thalia_varn|Professor Thalia Varn]]

Entry

The main entrance is staffed by a guard at all hours. The guard does not check credentials or ask for names. Instead, each person entering engages in a short choreographed duel — a fixed sequence of moves, unique to each student, established at enrollment and expected to be maintained. The sequence is brief enough that it takes less than a minute. It is demanding enough that students who haven't kept up their form notice immediately. Visitors who are not enrolled are paired with a generic sequence at the guard's discretion; it is always harder than it looks.

This is not considered unusual by anyone who has been here more than a week.

Facilities

A fortified structure that was designed to take punishment and pass that expectation on to its students. The main practice yard is sunken slightly below grade so that noise carries up and out rather than across campus. It does not fully work. The weapon racks along the interior corridors are restocked weekly; this is the only department where maintenance requests for "damaged equipment" are filed in bulk.

Small observation towers at each corner of the yard are used for archery drills and thrown-weapon practice. The range markers on the ground have been repainted over old range markers so many times that no one is certain what the original distances were.

Culture

The Fighters Department has the Academy's highest re-enrollment rate and the second-highest disciplinary incident rate (after the Barbarians Department, which has held the top position for eleven consecutive years). Students here tend to form strong cohort loyalties early and maintain them. The department's alumni association has more members than any other, and they stay in contact.

Professor Varn runs a department that is demanding but not cruel. She believes in clear standards, immediate feedback, and the kind of structured competition that makes people better rather than resentful. Students who wash out of the Fighters Department are often surprised to find she writes them a letter afterward.

Relationship with the [[dueling_society|Arcane Dueling Society]]

The Dueling Society draws heavily from the Fighters Department's upper years. Varn supports it. She sits on its oversight committee and has vetoed exactly three proposed matches in the last decade, all three on safety grounds, and two of the three students who were vetoed thanked her for it later.
