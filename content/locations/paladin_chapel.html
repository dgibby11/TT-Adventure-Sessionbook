Overview

The Paladins Department's attached chapel is where oaths are formally taken and renewed. The space is designed for exactly this purpose — stone floors, a central dais, twelve carved alcoves along the side walls each holding a votive lamp. The lamps are always lit. The smell is incense and polished stone and something faintly metallic that no one has traced to a source.

The chapel is twelve degrees off true north. This is visible from the alignment of the windows relative to the morning sun, and students who know what to look for will recognize that the off-alignment is deliberate — the building is positioned to face the [[memorial_garden|Memorial Garden]] at a specific angle, not toward any cardinal point. [[commander_orvyn|Commander Orvyn]] has never commented on this in any recorded context.

The West Wall

A memorial plaque on the west wall has been defaced — not removed, not replaced, defaced. The inscription under the damage is partially readable. A department name is legible. A year that ends in 3 is legible. The name in the center has been scored out completely.

DM Only

The plaque commemorates a paladin student from Year 43 — the one whose oath broke mid-semester in a way that the department classified internally as an "oath violation incident" and reported to administration as "voluntary withdrawal." The student was not withdrawn voluntarily. The plaque was defaced by a faculty member, not a student, two weeks after the incident. [[commander_orvyn|Orvyn]] knows this. He was not yet department head at the time. He has since made a decision that what happened was a failure of procedure, not of the department's values, and that leaving the plaque in its current state is an honest acknowledgment of that. He has not explained this to anyone.

The twelve-degree alignment was in the original building plans. The plans were drawn by someone who knew where the Garden would be. The Garden was established in Year 9. The Paladins building was completed in Year 7. The alignment was calculated in advance.
