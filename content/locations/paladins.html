Location
South-central campus; fortified tower with attached chapel
Focus
Oath-based magic, melee combat, divine auras, protective ability
Known for
Most formal culture on campus; also the most internally argumentative about what "formal" means

Facilities

A fortified tower with a chapel at ground level and training halls above. The heraldic banners along the upper walls represent every oath tradition currently taught — and one that isn't. The third banner from the east wall bears a crest that no current curriculum acknowledges. Faculty answer questions about it with a consistent brevity that suggests the brevity is intentional.

The armory is kept in immaculate order and inspected weekly. Students are expected to maintain their equipment to a standard that the inspection confirms. Students who fail inspection are not punished; they are required to assist with the next inspection, which is considered worse.

Oath Traditions

The Paladins Department teaches five oath traditions: Devotion, Ancients, Vengeance, Glory, and Watchers. The selection of traditions is not arbitrary — it reflects which oaths have sufficient doctrinal clarity to be taught academically without the Academy endorsing their specific theology. This distinction matters to the faculty. It matters very much. Discussions about it happen regularly. They are never resolved.

Culture

The Paladins Department takes argument seriously as an academic discipline. Students here are expected to be able to articulate the precise basis of their oath, the limits of its obligations, and the correct response when those limits are tested. Students who cannot do this in writing, in debate, and in practical examination are not considered to have mastered the material regardless of their combat performance.

This produces graduates who are formidable in every sense of the word and, occasionally, graduates who have spent four years developing an extremely principled position on a question no one else finds interesting.
