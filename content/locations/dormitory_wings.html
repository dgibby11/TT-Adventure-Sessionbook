Overview

The dormitory section of the Main Complex occupies the north wing and houses primarily first and second-year students. Third and fourth-year students increasingly move to department housing, either by preference or because their department makes it practical. The three wings — east, west, and center — each hold roughly a hundred students and have developed distinct reputations that predate any of the students currently in them.

The Three Wings

The west wing has the best heating system in the building. This is an observable fact, not a reputation, and first-year students learn it before the first cold week. The center wing has neither notable heat nor any of the other characteristics attributed to the east wing, and is consequently the most sought after — students understand it to be the stable option, which it is, and value this accordingly. The east wing is allegedly haunted. This reputation has persisted for at least fifteen years in the informal record and the formal record does not acknowledge it.

East Wing

The haunting claim centers on the third-floor corridor, specifically a stretch of hallway where the floorboards are colder than the surrounding area and where students report waking from unusual sleep at consistent times between two and three in the morning. No formal investigation has been conducted. The administration's position is that the building is old and old buildings have idiosyncrasies.

DM Only

The east wing cold corridor sits directly above a section of the building's original foundation that was modified during construction. The modification is in the original blueprints, which are in the restricted section of the [[old_library|Old Library]], and it shows a sealed passage that connects to something below the main complex that is not on any current map. The passage is still there. The cold comes from below it. The waking at 2–3am is a response to low-level magical resonance that has been building since Year 51, correlating with increased activity on the [[ritual_floor|ritual floor]].

Students who sleep in the east wing third-floor corridor occasionally have dreams they don't remember during the day but which share consistent visual elements — stone floors, geometric patterns, and a sound like something moving in a large space — with each other. None of them have compared notes yet. The DM should track whether this becomes a player-visible thread.
