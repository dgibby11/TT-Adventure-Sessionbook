Location
South-central campus; adjacent to the memorial paths
Focus
Divine magic, healing, radiant channeling, faith traditions
Known for
The best healthcare access on campus; the most sealed rooms of any department

Entry

The main entrance is a wrought iron gate set into the outer arch. It has no visible lock and no handle. It opens in response to a short, silent, whispered prayer — any sincere prayer to any divine tradition, in any language, to any power recognized by the department as legitimate. The faculty have never published a list of what qualifies. Students report that the gate is less forgiving of rote recitation than it is of genuine intent, though this is anecdotal.

Non-students entering for use of the healing ward use a separate entrance on the east side, which is staffed during posted hours and simply unlocked.

Facilities

A tall, vaulted sanctuary hall with smaller chapels branching from the nave — nine in total, each consecrated to a different divine tradition. The stone floors are worn smooth along the central aisle by decades of ritual movement. The candlelight is constant; the faculty's position is that electric lanterns are, at minimum, in poor taste.

The department also maintains a practical healing ward that is available to all Academy students and faculty during posted hours. This is considered the department's most concrete contribution to campus life by everyone who has used it after a Barbarians Department sparring session.

The Sealed Chapels

Three of the nine chapel alcoves have been sealed. The dedication plaques removed, the doors mortared shut. Current faculty describe this as "decommissioning of traditions no longer taught here." Students who press for more information are told that some traditions are no longer taught for good reasons, which is a sentence designed to end conversations.

Culture

The Clerics Department draws students from across the faith spectrum and maintains a deliberately non-denominational administration philosophy. In practice, the faculty tend to have strong opinions about each other's traditions and manage these opinions with varying degrees of success during faculty meetings. The inter-faith argument record at FAIL Academy's Clerics Department is, by any measure, competitive.

Students here tend to be either very certain of their path or very uncertain, with little middle ground. Both groups graduate well. The certain ones become effective practitioners; the uncertain ones become excellent theorists who ask the questions the certain ones stopped asking.
