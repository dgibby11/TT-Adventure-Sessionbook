Overview

The River Rauvin runs along the northern edge of the Academy campus, visible from several upstairs windows of the Main Complex and audible in the northern dormitories on quiet nights. It is cold, fast, and beautiful in the way that things are beautiful when they would kill you without much effort.

The river is both a boundary and a route. Downstream (southwest) leads toward [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]]. Upstream (northeast) leads toward the [[nether_mountains|Nether Mountains]] and, beyond them, into territories not covered by official maps. The [[expedition_club|Expedition Volunteers]] have mapped the bank for approximately four miles in each direction from campus; their notes are in The Log.

Wildlife and Hazards

  • [[wolves|Wolves]] — Pack territory follows the eastern bank for approximately three miles north. Avoid at dusk and dawn.
  • River Predators — Giant pike and other large aquatic creatures have been spotted in the deeper sections; nothing confirmed larger than CR 1. Probably.
  • Current — The spring melt makes the river crossings genuinely dangerous without the bridge. Three students have been swept downstream in the last decade. All recovered. None chose to repeat the experience.
  • The North Bank Past Mile 4 — The [[expedition_club|Expedition Volunteers]] stopped mapping here. Their notes say only "not recommended." No details given.

Uses

  • Fishing (common student pastime; results variable)
  • Travel to [[silverymoon|Silverymoon]] by river — downstream, 2 days with a boat, 3-4 without
  • Field exercise terrain — northern boundary exercises often incorporate the bank
  • The Faculty Road bridge is the only reliable crossing point; control of the bridge matters in emergencies

The North Ferry Crossing

Expeditions heading toward the [[nether_mountains|Nether Mountains]] use the rope ferry approximately three miles upstream of the Faculty Road bridge. It is wider, deeper, and faster at this crossing — the Faculty Road bridge is built where the river narrows; the ferry crossing is not. The flat-bottomed ferry is wide enough for a loaded cart and is operated by Brec, an old human who has run this crossing every day for thirty years. He opens at dawn and closes at full dark, no exceptions. He is polite about it and entirely immovable.

Brec knows most repeat travelers by face. He does not write down who crosses, but he tracks it in his head. A half-mile upstream of the ferry, the remains of a stone bridge pier stand mid-current — old work, two centuries at least, nothing connecting to either bank. Brec has never had an explanation for it and stopped expecting one.

After dark: The ford a half-mile downstream of the ferry is passable but adds two feet of cold fast current and a slippery stone bottom. Brec will tell you this without recommending it.

DM Only

Mile 4 north: the Expedition Volunteers stopped mapping because they found something. A section of the north bank shows signs of deliberate clearing and recent fire — within the last two months. Someone has been camping there, repeatedly, in numbers larger than a scouting party. They haven't made contact with the campus. They have been watching it. Tracks indicate they have been watching the [[fail_chamber|FAIL Chamber]] access point specifically.

The underwater section below the Faculty Road bridge has a submerged tunnel entrance. It's been there since the Academy was built. It doesn't appear on any maps except [[silas_morne|Silas's]] private ledger, where it appears as a small notation next to an entry about an unusually large order of rope in Year 12.
