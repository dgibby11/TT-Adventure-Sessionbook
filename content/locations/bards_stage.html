Overview

The main performance stage of the Bards Department is a resonance chamber — not metaphorically, but in the technical sense of a space designed to amplify and shape sound through architectural treatment. The [[bards|building]] has an enchantment that is most active here. Sound behaves incorrectly. A whisper from the back of the stage reaches the front wall before it should, measured by time. Multiple students have measured this. The measurements are consistent.

The Acoustic Anomaly

The anomaly is not subtle once you know to look for it. Sound in this room arrives at slightly different times than it should depending on position. Faculty use the chamber for certain assessments that require acoustic precision. Students use it for other things — the rooms of the department that have the best natural reverb for practice, the corners of the stage where sound from outside the building arrives distorted in ways that are sometimes usable, the alcoves where you can hear two separate conversations from across the room simultaneously.

What Students Know

The general understanding is that the building has "weird acoustics" that the Bards Department has learned to use. This is accurate and incomplete. [[maestro_lira|Maestro Lira]] has never explained the enchantment. She describes the acoustic behavior as "the room doing what rooms do when they've been treated correctly," which satisfies no one but has been accepted as the available answer.

DM Only

The resonance enchantment extends the room's acoustic range under specific conditions. Using the [[resonance_tuning_fork|tuning fork]] at the back of the stage produces a harmonic that temporarily extends the range to include the Main Complex courtyard — meaning anyone in this room, under those conditions, can hear whatever is being said in normal tones in the courtyard, and vice versa. Lira knows this. She stopped using the fork eleven years ago after a conversation in the courtyard she was not supposed to hear. She has not described what she heard.
