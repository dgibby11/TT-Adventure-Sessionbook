Overview

The High Forest is ancient in the way that means something has had a very long time to accumulate whatever it accumulates. It predates human settlement in the North by thousands of years and has outlasted several civilisations that tried to clear, cultivate, or annex it. The trees near the edge are enormous and old. Deeper in, they are larger. Accounts of what the trees look like at the forest's heart are inconsistent because the accounts are from people who came back, and people who came back tend to have had an experience that makes precise botanical observation secondary.

Relation to the Academy

The Academy's southwest field exercise sites extend to within half a mile of the forest edge. Students on perimeter exercises are briefed to maintain that half-mile buffer. Faculty supervision is required for any exercise that takes students closer. This rule has been broken roughly once per term since the Academy's founding. Outcomes have varied.

Several Academy field study papers reference specimens recovered from the forest edge. The field study papers do not describe how the specimens were recovered. The [[expedition_club|Expedition Volunteers]] have their own notes on this.

Known Edge Features

  • The Standing Stones: A circle of grey stones approximately twenty minutes inside the treeline. Pre-Academy, possibly pre-human. They are warm to the touch regardless of weather. Students dare each other to spend a night there. No one who has done so has described it as uneventful.
  • The Stream Fork: A clear-water stream that exits the forest and runs toward the [[river_rauvin|River Rauvin]]. Good water source. Creatures that drink from it upstream of the forest edge report tasting something that isn't water. Creatures that drink from it inside the forest edge report not drinking from it again.
  • Elf Contact: Wood elves from an interior settlement occasionally appear at the forest edge without explanation and without prior contact. They have been observed watching the campus from the treeline at least three times in the past year. They have not made contact. They have not appeared hostile.

Common Encounters (Edge)

  • [[wolves|Wolf packs]] — territory overlaps with the forest edge; frequent
  • Giant owls — nocturnal, non-aggressive unless approached
  • Fey creatures (pixies, sprites) — unpredictable; not necessarily hostile; definitely unreliable
  • Displacer beasts — rare; sightings near the forest edge have increased in the last two terms

DM Only

The wood elves watching from the treeline are from a settlement called Sharawood's Rest. They know about the Academy. They have known about it for seventy years. They were aware of the Lost Year (Year 34). They chose not to intervene. They are watching to see whether the current situation develops in a direction that changes that calculus.

The Standing Stones are a Weave-anchor point. Someone has been using them recently — within months. The use is not destructive, but it is the kind of use you would notice if you were the wood elves and you had been watching for seventy years. This is part of why they're watching more closely now.

The [[displacer_beast|Displacer Beast]] increase near the edge is being driven by something deeper in the forest that has disrupted their territory. The elves know what it is. They're not going to volunteer it.
