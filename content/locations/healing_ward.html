Overview

The healing ward is the Clerics Department's practical training facility and the one space in the building with a dedicated entrance that bypasses the main iron gate. The east-side entrance opens on a latch; no prayer, no consecration required. The administration specified this when the ward was built — healing is a service, not a rite, and access to it should not require an act of faith from the person arriving injured.

The ward itself is clean, well-lit by a row of shuttered windows on the south wall, and organized along twelve treatment beds with individual curtains. It is always calmer than the rest of the building and consistently somewhat quieter than the foot traffic through it would suggest it should be. The quiet has never been formally attributed to anything.

Population

Students from all departments end up here, with the [[barbarians|Barbarians Department]] representing a disproportionate share of visits. The student staff is trained to treat the ward as a professional context: information exchanged here about what happened and how stays here. Several students have tested this arrangement. It appears to hold.

DM Only

The ward's unusual quiet is a side effect of a low-level calming consecration that [[archcleric_senna|Senna]] installed in Year 36 after a patient went into shock during a difficult treatment. The consecration is real but minor — it doesn't compel anything, it simply makes the space easier to be in when in pain. She has not mentioned this to current students because it would prompt questions about what other consecrations exist in the building, and that conversation has a direction she does not want it to go.

The ward records are the most complete medical records on campus. They include injuries that departments would not have reported through official channels. If players have access to the records and know what they're looking for, the ward history for the last decade is a reliable timeline of departmental incidents the official record does not contain.
