Elevation
~3,000–5,000 ft — lower foothills approaching the Nether Mountains
Access
~10 miles north of the [[river_rauvin|River Rauvin]] crossing; 2 miles below the active expedition site
Campsite
Used by prior Academy expeditions; fire ring, tent stake holes, strung rope between boulders

The staging ground for any ascent into the [[nether_mountains|Nether Mountains]]. The previous expedition campsite is still usable — minimally maintained, no supplies, but a serviceable fire ring and some structural rope still rigged between boulders. Someone scratched the year of each expedition into a flat rock near the fire ring. Two years. Both crossed out.

Terrain

Wooded lower slopes give way to open boulder fields as the elevation rises. The treeline is a distinct line — above it, the landscape opens entirely and the wind has nothing to break it. A snowmelt stream cuts through the campsite area, running cold and clear year-round. The boulder fields above the tree line create complex overlapping sight lines; you can never be fully certain what you can and can't see at range.

Flora

Mountain heather and scrubby juniper dominate the lower approaches. Hardy wildflowers in sheltered hollows during the growing season — they close at dusk and can be used as a crude indicator of disturbance. Above the treeline: lichen and cold-hardy moss on shadowed boulder faces, nothing else.

Fauna

Marmots are the most reliable indicator of activity in the boulder fields. Their alarm whistle — one sharp note — carries far and is instinctively understood by anything predatory in the area. Mountain goats keep to the high scree and are rarely seen, though their tracks cross the approach paths regularly. Cave bats in the rock overhangs emerge at dusk. At the treeline edge, the usual northern woodland birds; above it, silence except for wind and the occasional raptor.

Signs of Prior Use

The campsite shows two seasons of expedition use. Both were Academy expeditions; both departed without filing completion reports. The fire ring is cold. The rope between the boulders is weathered but holds. A flat rock near the fire ring has two dates scratched into it by different hands. Both are crossed out with a single horizontal line.

DM Only

The crossed-out dates correspond to the two prior expeditions that abandoned the Nether Mountains site mid-season. The second crossing-out was made by a different hand than the date itself — someone came back to the campsite after their own expedition and struck it out before leaving. This is a detail Brec the ferryman can confirm: one member of that group crossed back south alone, two days after the rest of the party.

The night curiosities (cairn appearing, light on the ridge) can be orc scouts monitoring the approach routes, or something older using the boulder fields. Leave it ambiguous unless the session warrants development.
