What It Is

The Dead Hours is a student-run underground fight club and gambling den that has operated in the basement below the Food Court for nearly a decade. It has a ring, spectator seating (rough), a bar (illicit), and a rotating roster of student fighters. Betting is conducted in gold only — no [[academy_credits|Academy Credits]] accepted, because the records would be traceable. The Upper-Classmen run the operation, take a fifteen-percent house cut, and ensure that anyone who threatens to go to faculty has a very bad week.

The Environment

The sub-basement is reached via a service hatch behind the east wall serving counter of the [[food_court|Food Court]], accessible only after midnight when the kitchen closes. There are approximately forty feet of utility corridor before the space opens up. Someone has draped it in old canvas and strung up alchemical light orbs. It is, by student accounts, much better lit than you'd expect. The bar serves three things, all of them wrong.

Black Market Services

The Dead Hours is also the primary access point for the [[academy_credits|black market healing potion]] trade. A different broker operates from the ring most Fridays, taking gold only. Current broker is Oryn Saltwick, a third-year Bard. He is relaxed, cheerful, and works for the Upper-Classmen.

The Rules

Fights are three rounds, no magic, first to yield or fall. Fighters keep fifty percent of their bout pool. The Upper-Classmen keep fifteen. The rest goes back to the house for operating costs. There have been two serious injuries in eight years, both dealt with through the Academy's medical services under cover stories of "training accidents." No deaths. The organizers are genuinely careful about this — dead students end operations.

DM Only — Access & Use

Getting into the Dead Hours requires impressing or befriending a current member. Once in, PCs have access to:

  • Black market healing potions (100 gp each, no cap, Oryn Saltwick)
  • Rumor trading — this is where students talk about things they saw in the restricted sections of the [[old_library|Old Library]] or [[underground_storage|underground]], often drunk enough to be honest
  • A potential fight income stream — PCs could compete for gold
  • Access to the Upper-Classmen (who would normally be hard to approach)

The sub-basement itself: It connects to a maintenance passage that runs under the Food Court and eventually reaches a sealed door. That door leads further into the [[underground_storage|underground infrastructure]]. The seal is relatively new — within the last three years.
