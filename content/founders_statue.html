Location
Central campus — the junction of the three main campus paths
Depicts
One of the Academy's original founders (the dedication plaque does not specify which)
Age
Older than the plinth it stands on by at least a decade; predates the official dedication

Physical Description

A tall stone figure on a raised plinth, carved in a style that predates the surrounding architecture by several generations. The stone is worn smooth — features slightly indistinct in a way that is more erosion than time should fully account for. The figure is standing, arms at sides, looking east. The dedication plaque at the base is from Year 7 and does not specify which founder is depicted. This has been the subject of occasional academic interest. Nothing conclusive has been published.

Campus Significance

The statue sits where it does because it marks the geometric center of the original campus layout — or near enough to it that the discrepancy is generally attributed to survey error. Students walk past it dozens of times a week without stopping. It is not prominent in marketing materials for the Academy. It is prominent in the [[secret_society|Secret Society]]'s ritual geography.

DM Only

The statue was not built for this plinth. It arrived in Year 7 from somewhere else on campus — or somewhere off campus. The founding records for Year 7 contain a budget entry for "statue installation and preparation" but no origin listed. The plinth was constructed specifically to receive it.

The three sets of initials carved into the back of the plinth (A.V., I.O., B.A.) predate the plaque. Someone carved them before the statue arrived — or while it was being prepared for installation. The initials are not visible from the front; you have to go behind the plinth to find them.

The abjuration aura is not a protective ward. It is a recording anchor — a form of magical notation that captures and holds the nature of significant events that occur near it. The anchor has been active for as long as any magical survey has examined it. Whatever it has recorded, it is not accessible through any method currently in the Wizards Department library.

Ritual use: The [[ninth_thesis|Ninth Thesis]] rituals conducted here during [[session_2]] disturbed something in the anchor. Players who are magically sensitive may begin detecting a residual impression — fragments of something witnessed, not a message but an accumulation.
