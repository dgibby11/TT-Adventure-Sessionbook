Description

Three volumes in the sealed alcove on the Wizards Tower second floor. No author listed in any of them. No acquisition date stamped. No subject heading — not a missing subject heading, but a label field that was left blank. The alcove they occupy is sealed with a standard ward that most advanced Wizards students can bypass; the texts are not themselves warded.

The three volumes are written in three different hands. They cover, between them, subjects that the Wizards Department does not teach — specifically, a theory of plane-boundary anchoring that is not in published magical taxonomy, and two subjects that don't appear in any published taxonomic system at all. One of those subjects has no name in the volumes, only a symbol that repeats across all three texts in all three hands.

DM Only

The three texts were written by the three founders. They represent the working notes for the ritual that established the Academy's foundational enchantment — the anchor on the [[ritual_floor|ritual floor]] — and as such contain more accurate information about what that ritual was designed to do than anything currently in the [[restricted_floor|restricted library collection]]. The repeated symbol is the designation for the entity that the [[archival_prayer_text|prayer text]] in the Clerics chapel is designed to invoke: the founders included it in their notes as a warning about what the plane-boundary weakening at this location had already attracted, and their ritual was partly designed to manage that attraction.

The alcove is sealed because [[prof_aldous_fenwick|Prof. Fenwick]] found the texts in Year 49, read enough of them to know they were significant, and warded the alcove while he decided what to do. He has not decided. He has not told anyone. He is seventy-three years old and the thing he is afraid of is making the wrong decision about something this consequential. He keeps not deciding.
