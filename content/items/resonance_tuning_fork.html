Description

A tuning fork that appears standard at a glance. The pitch it resonates at is not on the standard pitch chart — not a common frequency, not a variant of a known one, but a specific value that doesn't appear in any instrument calibration reference available in the department. When struck in normal circumstances, the resonance is faintly wrong in a way that experienced musicians identify immediately and cannot quite describe.

When struck near the resonance enchantment in the [[bards_stage|main stage]], the effect is more specific: the acoustic range of the room extends temporarily to include the Main Complex courtyard. Anything said in normal tones in either location during the window is audible in the other.

DM Only

[[maestro_lira|Lira]] has not used the fork in eleven years. She stopped after hearing something during an extension that she did not intend to trigger — a conversation in the courtyard between two people she recognized, about something she was not supposed to know, that changed her understanding of the Academy's administrative structure significantly. She has not acted on what she heard. She has not described what she heard. She keeps the fork because it is valuable and because destroying it would mean explaining why she destroyed it, which would require describing what she heard.

The fork was not made by the Bards Department. It was made by someone who understood the specific resonance enchantment in the building and designed an instrument calibrated to its harmonic structure. This implies the fork was made by someone who built the enchantment or had access to its specifications. The fork predates [[maestro_lira|Lira's]] tenure at the department.
