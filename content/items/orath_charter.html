The founding charter was authored by Isolde Orath, second of the three founders. It specified non-discriminatory admission, perpetual scholarship funding, and a prohibition on requiring students to demonstrate abilities in circumstances designed to harm them. That prohibition now exists in current policy in language so qualified as to be nearly unrecognizable from its original form.

Three letters from Orath to Brennan Ashcroft, written in the months before her disappearance, are held under administrative seal in the restricted archive. They have not been made available for research. Their contents are unknown.
