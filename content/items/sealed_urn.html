Description

A ceramic urn approximately fourteen inches tall, sitting against the north wall of the ritual floor chamber. Sealed with dark wax bearing an impression that matches a section of the geometric pattern on the chamber floor. The seal appears original and has not been disturbed. The urn is heavier than an empty urn of this size and material should be, by a margin that is noticeable when lifted. Contents cannot be determined without breaking the seal.

The wax has not deteriorated in a way consistent with its age. The ceramic has settled dust. The wax has not.

DM Only

The urn contains the ash of three binding components from the original founding ritual — the consumed elements of the first activation of the geometric pattern. The founders kept the ash because the ritual specified it, not because they understood why. Breaking the seal does not destroy the components but it does interact with the residual enchantment in the pattern: opening the urn in the chamber partially reactivates the pattern, specifically the outer ring, which produces a diagnostic state rather than a full activation. In the diagnostic state, the pattern shows which activation points are still holding and which have been disrupted — it functions as a map of the enchantment's current integrity.

The wax impression matching the floor pattern is not coincidental. It is a key. The seal was designed to respond to the pattern's resonance: in a fully dormant chamber, the wax is cold to the touch; in the current partially-active state, the wax is faintly warm, which players in the chamber can detect with a passive Perception of 14 or higher.
