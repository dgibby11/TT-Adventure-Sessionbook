Description

A standard student notebook found on one of the equipment benches in the [[artificers_forge|forge]]. The cover is plain, no name. The content describes failed experiments — incorrect results, unexpected reactions, materials that didn't behave as predicted. The notation is technically competent. The experiments described are in the right format for Artificers coursework. On close inspection the results are not failures.

The "failures" are calibrations. Each recorded "incorrect" result is a step in a sequence that, when read across the full notebook, describes a precise series of adjustments toward an end product. The end product is not named. The final calibration in the sequence was apparently successful. The notebook ends there.

DM Only

The notebook belongs to a student who graduated in Year 54 — a member of the Artificers cohort who left without unusual incident and is not in any current security record. The final calibration produced a specific alchemical compound that, when applied to certain magical wards, disrupts the authentication layer without triggering the alarm function. This is a very specialized thing to have developed as a student project. The student's current whereabouts are unknown. The [[thornwick_consortium|Thornwick Consortium]] employs people with exactly this skill set.

[[tinkerer_dunn|Dunn]] has not been able to determine what the notebook was calibrating toward. This is partly because the resonance dampeners in the forge reduce detection accuracy, and partly because identifying the end product requires expertise in ward-disruption that he does not have and would not expect to need.
