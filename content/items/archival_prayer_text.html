Description

A bound volume on the secondary lectern of the main unsealed chapel. The script is not in any divine tradition currently taught in the Clerics Department — the letterforms are recognizable as a variant of a pre-Sundering writing system, and the language is related to a liturgical dialect that predates the current faith traditions by at least two centuries. Faculty describe it as archival. Students who have spent significant time in the chapel have tried to read it with varying degrees of success.

The volume does not appear to be in good condition and is in better condition than it should be. The binding has been replaced at some point but the pages are older than the binding.

DM Only

The text is an invocation — a ritual approach to an entity that predates every church tradition the Clerics Department recognizes. [[archcleric_senna|Senna]] has partially translated it. The entity it describes is not named in a way that matches any deity in the current divine taxonomy. It is described by function: a thing that answers when called from a sealed space, that cannot be compelled but can be bargained with, that has a specific interest in locations where the boundary between planes is structurally weakened.

The sealed chapels were sealed because this entity was answering petitions that were addressed to other deities. It was not causing harm in any direct sense. It was simply answering when it should not have been the thing that answered. What it wanted from the exchange is the more important question, and Senna's translation does not reach that section.
