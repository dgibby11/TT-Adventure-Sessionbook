Setting Summary

FAIL Academy is a magical combat academy built on systems that were never fully understood, are no longer fully controlled, and may have been corrupt from the beginning. The school hides dangerous things, runs real off-campus operations, and treats students as expendable resources. The PCs are among the few who have survived incidents they probably were not expected to survive — and they are starting to notice a pattern.

Core Themes

Hidden Dangers
Sealed underground horrors, dangerous artifacts, unstable infrastructure
Active Conspiracy
Secret rituals, hidden factions, institutional secrecy, forbidden knowledge
Loss of Control
Malfunctioning systems, containment failures, staff manipulation, students caught in crossfire

What the Players Know

Reminders for mid-session clarification — safe to surface to players:

  • Something is wrong with the school; dangerous creatures are hidden beneath campus; the administration covers things up; staff may not act of their own volition. (Session 1)
  • A Secret Society exists; some students know more about the school's true nature; hidden academic doctrines and rituals exist. (Session 2)
  • The school uses students in real danger for exercises; off-campus field sites exist; powerful artifacts are in play; portals can fail and release dangerous creatures; the dinosaur breach is now known campus-wide. (Session 3)

DM Only — Established Canon (permanent world truths)

  • Sealed underground areas beneath campus contain dangerous creatures.
  • Students are routinely placed in situations they are not fully informed about.
  • The administration actively covers up dangerous incidents; staff are manipulated or complicit.
  • The [[secret_society]] conducts rituals near the [[founders_statue]]; the statue is significant to the school's true nature.
  • The school operates remote field sites with real danger disguised as simulations; teleportation portals are unstable and can breach.
  • Multiple artifacts exist with unclear origins; creatures can be influenced or triggered by them.
  • Containment systems are imperfect and failing. The PCs are now notable, known campus-wide.
