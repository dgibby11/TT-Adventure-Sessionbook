Overview

The original founding document of the Academy, distinct from [[orath_charter|Orath's Charter Documents]] which cover the Academy's legal incorporation. This is the internal agreement — what [[aldric_voss|Voss]], [[isolde_orath|Orath]], and [[brennan_ashcroft|Ashcroft]] agreed between themselves about what they were building, and what obligations that building carried. The public record describes an academy for adventurers. This document describes something more specific.

Contents (Summary)

The Charter establishes three obligations the founders accepted as conditions of the Academy's founding: maintenance of the ritual anchor below the main complex; management of the plane-boundary weakening at this location that predated their construction; and stewardship of the relationship with the entity that had already been answering from this site. The Academy's educational function is real and was their genuine intention. It is also, per the Charter, the practical cover for the three obligations. Students are not in the Charter.

The Third Obligation

The third obligation — stewardship of the entity that was answering from the site — is described in four dense pages that are the most heavily annotated section of the document. The annotations are in all three founders' hands. They did not agree on what the third obligation required. The document reflects this disagreement without resolving it.

DM Only

The Charter is the single most informative document the players can find. It explains the ritual floor, the sealed chapels, the resonance enchantment, and the founding intent behind the nine-compass sites in a single source. It also clarifies that [[headmistress_dowe|Dowe's]] knowledge of the secret society is not the full picture: the Society knows about the founding ritual but has misidentified what it was for. They believe it was a binding to constrain something. It was actually a binding to acknowledge something — a significant distinction in terms of what happens if the anchor is disrupted.

Finding the Charter requires access to the [[restricted_floor|restricted floor]] and navigation to the Founding Documents section, which has had some of its contents removed. The Charter itself has not been removed. Whoever replaced the other documents either didn't find it or left it deliberately.
