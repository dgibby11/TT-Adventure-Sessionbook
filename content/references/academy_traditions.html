FAIL Academy marks the academic year with a fixed calendar of ceremonies, exams, and traditions — some dignified, some bizarre, most mandatory. Below is the full academic year as it is officially observed and, where relevant, as it is actually experienced.

The Academic Year

Term 1 (Autumn)
Weeks 1–12. Field exercises, classroom work, mid-term assessment.
Winter Recess
Three weeks. Campus reduced to essential staff. Strange things happen when the students leave.
Term 2 (Spring)
Weeks 13–24. Advanced curriculum, electives, end-of-year bracket.
FAIL Period
Final two weeks of Term 2. Practical and written examination season. Known informally as "Failure Week" or simply "The Period."
Summer Recess
Eight weeks. Students are encouraged to pursue independent study. Most go home.

The Opening Gauntlet

First week of each academic year. All new students face a week of assessments that are, officially, placement tests. Unofficially, they are the Academy's most efficient method for sorting students by what they'll actually do under pressure, rather than what their entrance applications suggest. The Gauntlet includes a written exam (Monday), a physical trial (Wednesday), a practical magic assessment (Thursday), and an unannounced combined scenario (Friday) that changes every year and is never described in advance.

Students who perform exceptionally in the Friday scenario are tracked. Where they are tracked to is not public information.

The Year-End Duel

Final event of the academic year, traditionally held the Saturday before FAIL Period begins. The [[dueling_society|Arcane Dueling Society's]] top-ranked students face off in a formal single-elimination bracket. Open to the full Academy community as a spectator event. [[headmistress_dowe|Headmistress Dowe]] always attends, always sits in the same seat, and always stays for the entire event, regardless of how long it runs.

The Year-End Duel trophy is a battered enchanted gauntlet with an indeterminate origin. It glows faintly when held by the winner. No one has studied why. The enchantment seems benign.

The Vigil of the Founders

Third night of Winter Recess. A small, formal ceremony at the [[memorial_garden|Founder's Memorial Garden]], attended by faculty, staff, and any students who remain on campus during the break. [[headmistress_dowe|Headmistress Dowe]] speaks briefly about the founding vision of the Academy. There is a candle-lighting component. No one leaves before the headmistress does.

The ceremony is brief, quiet, and older students note it feels different every year without being able to say exactly how.

Traditions (Unofficial)

  • The First Rope: New students are told, in some form, during their first week that they will need rope. Students who didn't buy rope often recall hearing this and dismissing it. Students who bought rope are never specific about why they needed it.
  • The Midnight Run: An informal tradition in which students attempt to cross the entire campus perimeter after midnight without being caught by the faculty watch. No official record. No official consequences. The faculty watch catches approximately one in three attempts and pretends not to recognise the students.
  • The Ledger: Every year, a graduating student leaves behind a list of tips for incoming students in a location that changes each year. Known as "The Ledger." Finding it is itself considered an initiation. Content ranges from practical to cryptic to, in at least two instances, appears to have been redacted.

DM Only

The Opening Gauntlet tracking: High performers on the Friday scenario are quietly flagged for a secondary assessment run by [[ellery_voss|Dr. Ellery Voss]]. The nature of this assessment and what it selects for is connected to the [[fail_chamber|FAIL Examination Chamber]] arc.

The Year-End Duel trophy: The gauntlet is not from the Academy's own collection. Its enchantment is older than the building. [[prof_fenwick|Prof. Fenwick]] has examined it once and declined to say what he found.

The Winter Recess "strange things": During the three-week winter break, faculty and staff with overnight obligations have reported sounds from the sub-basement levels that don't match anything scheduled. Two maintenance staff have refused specific overnight shifts without explanation. This has been quietly noted by [[silas_morne|Silas Morne]].
