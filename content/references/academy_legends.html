FAIL Academy is old enough to have accumulated its own mythology. Some of it is provably false. Some of it is probably false. Some of it has never been investigated closely enough to be sure.

The Whispering Archive

Somewhere in the [[old_library|Old Library]] — older students say on the third floor, though others insist the building doesn't have a third floor — there is a section of shelves known as the Whispering Archive. Books shelved there, according to the legend, will whisper back to you if you read them aloud in the right way. What they whisper is described variously as: the thing you most need to hear, the thing you least want to know, and — by one student, a Year 3 who left the Academy shortly after — "exactly what I had done and who else knew about it."

The Archive, if it exists, has never been officially listed in the library catalogue.

The Lost Year — Year 34

The Academy maintains detailed records going back to its founding — attendance rolls, examination results, field exercise logs, financial accounts. For exactly one academic year, the thirty-fourth year of operation, there are no records. The entry in the administrative archive for that year is a single line: "Records for Academic Year 34 are unavailable pending review." The review has apparently been pending for sixty-one years.

Students who ask faculty about Year 34 receive three types of response: those who deny it happened, those who change the subject with unusual speed, and [[prof_aldous_fenwick|Professor Fenwick]], who said once, and has not repeated, "Something was learned that year that has not yet been unlearned."

The Golden Cohort

Sixty-two years ago, a graduating class of twelve students — the highest-performing cohort in the Academy's history at that point — left the Academy. Within one year of graduation, all twelve were dead. The causes were varied: five in the field, two to illness, two under circumstances listed as "undetermined," one in what was classified as a tragic accident, and two whose deaths have no Academy record at all because they apparently ceased to appear in any documentation simultaneously, as though they had never existed.

No reunion was held for the Golden Cohort. The tradition of holding reunions was discontinued the same year they graduated. Reunions were reinstated fourteen years later, by which point no official connection was made.

The Last Student

The most persistent ghost story: a student who, at some point in the Academy's past, went down into the lower tunnels and did not come back. According to the legend, they are still there. Sometimes still studying. Sometimes not. Students doing late-night maintenance in the [[food_court|Food Court]] storage sometimes hear footsteps in the corridor below. Students doing anything in the [[underground_storage|deeper underground levels]] are simply told, unofficially, not to go in alone.

No student disappearance matching this description appears in the official records. The official records for certain periods are, of course, incomplete.

The Room That Moves

Some first-year students spend their entire first week unable to find certain rooms on the Academy map — rooms listed in the class schedule that other students seem to find without difficulty. The explanation offered by second-years is that "some rooms move." The explanation offered by faculty is that "the maps are occasionally updated and students sometimes use outdated versions." These explanations have coexisted for twenty years without either being proven or disproven.

DM Only — Legend Status

  • Whispering Archive: Real. Located through a concealed door in the [[old_library|Old Library]]'s second-floor north stacks. The books don't actually whisper — but the room has a Detect Thoughts effect keyed to anyone who reads aloud within it. [[headmistress_dowe|Headmistress Dowe]] knows about it and has sealed it. [[prof_aldous_fenwick|Prof. Fenwick]] knows where the seal is and has not opened it in twenty years.
  • The Lost Year: Real. [[headmistress_dowe|Dowe]] has the records. They document a catastrophic experiment by the [[secret_society|Secret Society's]] predecessor that resulted in the disappearance of an entire graduating class. The experiment was not entirely unsuccessful. This is what makes it complicated.
  • The Golden Cohort: Real, approximately. They were the previous iteration of whatever the current Secret Society is trying to accomplish. The "undetermined" deaths were not natural. The two students with no records were relocated. Where is your call.
  • The Last Student: There is someone in the underground. They are not a ghost. Whether they are a student is debatable. They have been there for approximately forty years.
  • The Room That Moves: Partly true. Three rooms in the Main Complex have an occasional spatial anomaly tied to the [[voss_imprint|Voss architectural imprint]]. On certain days — no clear pattern — they connect to somewhere else for approximately one hour. So far no one has come through from the other side. So far.
